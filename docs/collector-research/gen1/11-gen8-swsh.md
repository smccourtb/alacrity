# Gen 8 (Sword/Shield + DLC) Collector Research

Generation VIII is a **paradigm shift for collectors** -- Pokemon Sword/Shield (2019) and their Expansion Pass DLCs (Isle of Armor, June 2020; Crown Tundra, October 2020) introduced **Marks/Titles** (a permanent, randomized cosmetic layer that creates an entirely new axis of collector prestige), **Gigantamax forms** (12 Gen 1 species have unique Gmax transformations), **Dynamax Adventures** (the best shiny hunting method for legendary Pokemon at 1/100 with Shiny Charm), **Galarian forms** (11 Gen 1 species received regional variants), the **Wild Area** (the first open-world zone with weather-dependent spawns), and the controversial **"Dexit"** that excluded roughly half of all species from the game entirely.

**The headline features are Marks and Dynamax Adventures.** Marks are random titles applied to wild Pokemon at catch time that can never be added afterward -- a shiny Pokemon with a rare Personality Mark is the ultimate collector trophy, and mark hunting is a major community activity. Dynamax Adventures in the Crown Tundra DLC offer 1/100 shiny odds (with Shiny Charm) for legendary Pokemon, making them the single best shiny legendary method in the entire series. Both features are exclusive to Sword/Shield and cannot be replicated in any other game.

**SwSh is also the first game with the National Dex cut.** Only ~400 species were available at launch (expanded to ~664 with both DLCs), meaning many Gen 1 Pokemon simply cannot enter the game. Roughly 36 Gen 1 species are completely excluded. For the species that ARE available, SwSh offers an extraordinarily rich collecting environment with marks, Gmax forms, Galarian forms, rare balls from the Cram-o-matic, and multiple shiny methods.

For LGPE mechanics (catch combos, overworld shinies, Let's Go origin mark), see [10-lgpe.md](10-lgpe.md). This document assumes familiarity with Gen 7 systems and focuses on what Gen 8 adds or changes.

## Section A: Game-Wide Mechanics

### A.1 Marks and Titles (NEW -- CRITICAL FOR COLLECTORS)

Marks are the single most important new collector mechanic in Generation VIII. A mark is a special designation that can be randomly present on a wild Pokemon at the moment of capture. When equipped, a mark adds a **title** to the Pokemon's name in battle (e.g., "Pikachu the Sociable"). Marks are the first purely cosmetic, permanently attached, randomized collector attribute in the series.

#### A.1.1 Core Rules

- Marks are determined at the moment of encounter -- they are **not** influenced by catch method, ball type, or any player action
- A Pokemon either has a mark or it does not; this is fixed at spawn time
- Marks **cannot** be added, removed, or changed after catch
- A Pokemon can have at most **one** mark (though it can also have ribbons -- marks and ribbons are displayed in the same interface)
- Marks are visible in the summary screen under the "Ribbons" tab
- When a mark is equipped, the Pokemon's battle introduction displays its title

#### A.1.2 Eligibility

**CAN have marks:**
- Wild symbol encounters (overworld Pokemon)
- Wild random encounters (exclamation mark in grass)
- Fishing encounters
- Curry-attracted Pokemon (guaranteed Curry Mark)
- Galarian legendary birds (Crown Tundra roaming encounters) -- these are technically wild symbol encounters

**CANNOT have marks:**
- Max Raid Battle catches
- Dynamax Adventure catches
- Eggs / hatched Pokemon
- Gift Pokemon
- In-game trade Pokemon
- Static encounters (Zacian, Zamazenta, Eternatus, etc.)
- Event / Mystery Gift Pokemon

**Critical collector implication:** Only wild-caught Pokemon can have marks. This means marks are exclusively available for species that appear as wild encounters, and the most prestigious specimens (shiny + mark) can only come from wild catches, never from breeding or raids.

#### A.1.4 Mark Odds Breakdown

When encountering a wild Pokemon, the game rolls for marks in the following order. If any roll succeeds, that mark is assigned and no further rolls occur:

1. **Personality mark roll**: ~1/100 chance for any of the 28 personality marks (each individually ~1/2800)
2. **Time mark roll**: ~1/50 for the time-appropriate mark
3. **Weather mark roll**: ~1/50 for the weather-appropriate mark
4. **Fishing mark roll** (fishing only): ~1/25
5. **Uncommon mark roll**: ~1/50
6. **Rare mark roll**: ~1/1000
7. **Curry mark**: guaranteed if attracted via curry

**Approximate chance of ANY mark on a standard wild encounter:**
- Clear weather, no special conditions: roughly 1 in 12 encounters will have some mark
- Eligible weather + time: slightly higher due to more applicable rolls

**Approximate chance of a SPECIFIC personality mark:** ~1/2800

**Approximate chance of the Rare Mark ("the Recluse"):** 1/1000 -- the most prestigious wild mark due to extreme rarity

#### A.1.5 Mark Hunting as Collector Activity

Mark hunting is a significant community activity. The typical approach:

1. Identify a target species available as a wild encounter
2. Go to a location where it spawns reliably
3. Encounter, check for marks, catch or flee, repeat
4. The dream target: **shiny + rare mark** -- probability approximately 1/4,096,000 (1/4096 shiny x 1/1000 rare mark) without any bonuses

**Mark + Shiny probability discussion:**
- Shiny + ANY mark: roughly 1 in 50,000 encounters
- Shiny + specific personality mark: roughly 1 in 11,500,000 encounters
- Shiny + Rare Mark ("the Recluse"): roughly 1 in 4,096,000 encounters
- These are the rarest legitimately obtainable specimens in the series

**Important:** Marks persist through transfer to HOME and newer games. A marked Pokemon retains its title permanently.

#### A.2.3 Galarian Legendary Birds

The Galarian legendary birds are unique -- they are not simply regional forms of the originals but are described as distinct subspecies. They are encountered as roaming overworld Pokemon in the Crown Tundra story and must be chased across Galar:

- **Galarian Articuno**: roams in the Crown Tundra proper
- **Galarian Zapdos**: roams in the Wild Area (mainland Galar)
- **Galarian Moltres**: roams on the Isle of Armor

**Critical notes:**
- All three are **shiny locked** -- they cannot be shiny
- They ARE wild encounters (symbol encounters), so they **can have marks** -- one of the only legendary Pokemon in the series eligible for marks
- One of each per save file
- They use the same Pokedex entry as their Kanto counterparts (not separate species)

**The original Kanto Articuno, Zapdos, and Moltres are available separately** through Dynamax Adventures in the Crown Tundra (see A.4). The Galarian and Kanto forms are distinct encounters.

#### A.2.4 Evolution Mechanics in Galar

Like Alola, evolving in Galar defaults to the Galarian form for applicable species:
- Kanto Meowth evolved in Galar -> Kanto Persian (Meowth is unique -- Kanto Meowth evolves to Persian, Galarian Meowth evolves to Perrserker)
- Galarian Ponyta evolved in Galar -> Galarian Rapidash
- Galarian Farfetch'd + 3 crits in one battle -> Sirfetch'd (Kanto Farfetch'd has no evolution)

**Kanto Meowth exception:** Kanto Meowth cannot be caught wild in Galar. A special Gigantamax Meowth was distributed via early-purchase Mystery Gift. Kanto Meowth can also be obtained via in-game trade or transfer from HOME.

### A.3 Gigantamax Forms (NEW -- MAJOR)

Gigantamaxing is an enhanced form of Dynamaxing where compatible Pokemon not only grow giant but change form and gain a unique G-Max Move. The Gigantamax factor is a special attribute that can be present on a specimen -- not all members of a Gmax-capable species can Gigantamax; only those with the factor.

#### A.3.2 How to Obtain the Gigantamax Factor

**Method 1: Max Raid Battles (Base Game)**
- Gigantamax Pokemon appear in specific Max Raid dens in the Wild Area
- They have a **purple swirling beam** (rare den) rather than a standard red beam
- Gmax raids are uncommon and have lower catch rates than standard Max Raids
- Catch rate in Gmax raids: ~3% base rate for non-host players (much higher for host)
- Available for: Butterfree, Charizard, Pikachu (via LGPE save gift), Meowth (early Mystery Gift), Eevee (via LGPE save gift), Machamp, Gengar, Kingler, Lapras, Snorlax
- Promoted Gmax raids rotate through Wild Area News events

**Method 2: Max Soup (Isle of Armor DLC -- CRITICAL)**
- Available after completing Mustard's second trial at the Master Dojo
- Costs **3 Max Mushrooms** per use (found in Isle of Armor caves, respawn daily)
- Can give the Gigantamax factor to ANY compatible species
- **Toggleable** -- feeding Max Soup to a Pokemon that already has the Gmax factor REMOVES it
- Works on **any specimen** regardless of origin -- transferred, bred, shiny, whatever
- This is the primary method for collectors, as it removes RNG from Gmax acquisition
- **Exception:** Melmetal cannot use Max Soup (its Gmax form is event-exclusive)

**Method 3: Gift Pokemon**
- **Gmax Pikachu**: gift for having a Let's Go, Pikachu! save on the same Switch (received from a boy in Meetup Spot, Wild Area station)
- **Gmax Eevee**: gift for having a Let's Go, Eevee! save on the same Switch (same location)
- **Gmax Meowth**: early-purchase Mystery Gift (available until January 15, 2020)
- These gift Pokemon **cannot evolve** while they have the Gmax factor -- removing it with Max Soup allows evolution

#### A.3.3 Gigantamax and Shiny Status

- Gmax Pokemon from raids CAN be shiny (standard 1/4096 odds; Shiny Charm does NOT affect raid odds)
- Gmax Pokemon obtained from Max Soup are whatever the base specimen is -- if the Pokemon was shiny before Max Soup, it remains shiny with the Gmax factor
- The gift Gmax Pikachu, Eevee, and Meowth are **NOT shiny locked** in theory, but are fixed specimens with predetermined data

**Collector note:** The easiest path to a shiny Gmax Gen 1 Pokemon is to breed a shiny via Masuda Method and then use Max Soup. This bypasses the extremely low catch rates of shiny Gmax raids.

#### A.3.4 Collector Value of Gmax Forms

Gigantamax is exclusive to Sword/Shield -- the mechanic does not exist in any other game. While the Gmax factor persists on a Pokemon transferred to HOME, it has no functional effect outside SwSh. This makes Gmax forms a SwSh-exclusive prestige attribute.

The Gmax factor is **not visible** in HOME or other games as a distinct marker -- it is metadata that only activates in SwSh battles. For pure collectors, the value is in having a specimen that CAN Gigantamax when returned to SwSh, and in the aesthetic of the unique Gmax form during battle.

### A.4 Dynamax Adventures (Crown Tundra -- CRITICAL)

Dynamax Adventures are cooperative Max Raid gauntlets in the Max Lair (Crown Tundra). They are the **single best shiny hunting method for legendary Pokemon** in the entire series.

#### A.4.1 How They Work

1. Enter the Max Lair and choose a rental Pokemon (you do NOT use your own team)
2. Navigate a branching path of 3 consecutive Max Raid battles, picking up rental Pokemon along the way
3. The 4th and final battle is always against a **legendary or Ultra Beast**
4. After completing the adventure, you can choose to keep ONE Pokemon from any of the four battles
5. **Shiny status is not revealed until the end** -- Pokemon appear in their normal colors during the adventure and only show as shiny on the results screen

#### A.4.2 Shiny Odds

| Condition | Shiny Odds |
|-----------|-----------|
| Base (no Shiny Charm) | **1/300** (13.65/4096) |
| With Shiny Charm | **1/100** (40.96/4096) |

These are the best shiny odds for legendary Pokemon in any main series game. The 1/100 rate with Shiny Charm means a dedicated hunter will average a shiny legendary every ~100 adventures (each taking 15-25 minutes).

**Critical mechanic:** Shiny odds apply independently to EACH of the 4 Pokemon encountered in an adventure. Even non-legendary Pokemon encountered along the way have 1/100 (or 1/300) shiny odds. This means you have a reasonable chance of seeing a shiny in nearly every adventure session.

#### A.4.3 Gen 1 Legendaries in Dynamax Adventures

| Pokemon | Available? | Version | Notes |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------|
| Articuno (Kanto) | **Yes** | Both | Standard Dynamax Adventure boss |
| Zapdos (Kanto) | **Yes** | Both | Standard Dynamax Adventure boss |
| Moltres (Kanto) | **Yes** | Both | Standard Dynamax Adventure boss |
| Mewtwo | **Yes** | Both | Standard Dynamax Adventure boss |

All four Kanto legendaries are available in Dynamax Adventures and **can be shiny**. This is the recommended method for shiny Kanto legendary hunting.

**One-per-save restriction:** Once you catch and KEEP a specific legendary, it is removed from your Dynamax Adventure pool. You cannot encounter it again on that save file. **If you keep a non-shiny legendary, you lose the chance to hunt its shiny on that save.**

**Strategy for shiny legendary hunting:**
1. Enter the Dynamax Adventure and battle through to the legendary
2. Catch it (guaranteed catch with any ball -- you cannot fail)
3. On the results screen, check if the legendary is shiny
4. If shiny: keep it
5. If NOT shiny: **do not keep it** -- select a different Pokemon or select "Don't take any" to release the legendary back into the pool
6. Repeat until shiny

#### A.4.4 Notable Version Exclusives in Dynamax Adventures

While the Kanto legendaries are available in both versions, many other legendaries are version-locked:

- **Sword exclusive**: Xerneas, Groudon, Latios, Ho-Oh, Solgaleo, Dialga, Tornadus, Reshiram
- **Shield exclusive**: Yveltal, Kyogre, Latias, Lugia, Lunala, Palkia, Thundurus, Zekrom

**Both versions**: Mewtwo, Articuno, Zapdos, Moltres, Suicune, Rayquaza, Giratina, Cresselia, Heatran, and many others

#### A.4.5 Poke Ball Selection in Dynamax Adventures

All Pokemon caught in Dynamax Adventures are caught with a ball you select from your inventory. Since catch is guaranteed, **you can use any ball for aesthetic purposes**:
- Apricorn Balls, Beast Balls, Sport Balls, Safari Balls -- all work at 100% catch rate
- This is the ONLY method to easily obtain legendary Pokemon in rare ball types
- A Beast Ball or Sport Ball Mewtwo from Dynamax Adventures is a legitimate prestige specimen

### A.5 Wild Area and Weather System

The Wild Area is an open-world zone connecting Motostoke and Hammerlocke, featuring visible overworld Pokemon, weather-dependent encounters, and Max Raid dens. The DLC areas (Isle of Armor, Crown Tundra) use the same open-world mechanics.

#### A.5.1 Weather Types

Nine weather conditions exist, each affecting which Pokemon spawn:

| Weather | Effect on Encounters | Battle Effect | Mark |
|---------|---------------------|---------------|------|
| Clear | Normal spawns | None | None |
| Cloudy | Normal/Fighting/Poison/Fairy boosted | None | Cloudy Mark |
| Rain | Water/Bug/Electric/Dark boosted | Rain | Rainy Mark |
| Thunderstorm | Water/Electric/Dragon boosted | Rain | Stormy Mark |
| Snow | Ice boosted | Hail | Snowy Mark |
| Blizzard | Ice/Steel boosted | Hail | Blizzard Mark |
| Harsh Sunlight | Fire/Grass/Ground boosted | Sun | Dry Mark |
| Sandstorm | Ground/Rock/Steel boosted | Sandstorm | Sandstorm Mark |
| Fog | Psychic/Ghost/Dark/Fairy boosted | Misty Terrain | Misty Mark |

**Weather changes daily at midnight** based on the Switch system clock. Each Wild Area sub-zone has independent weather. Fog is only available after becoming Champion.

#### A.5.2 Overworld Encounters

Pokemon are visible in the overworld before contact. Two encounter types exist:
- **Symbol encounters** (visible Pokemon walking around): can have marks, level varies by area
- **Random encounters** (exclamation mark "!" in tall grass): can have marks, typically lower-level

Both encounter types are eligible for marks, making the Wild Area the primary mark hunting ground.

### A.6 Shiny Hunting Methods

SwSh offers multiple shiny hunting methods, each with different odds and applications.

#### A.6.1 Masuda Method (Breeding)

| Condition | Odds |
|-----------|------|
| Base (different-language parents) | ~1/683 (6/4096) |
| With Shiny Charm | ~1/512 (8/4096) |

Standard breeding method. Remains the most reliable shiny method for most species. Pokemon hatched from eggs **cannot have marks**.

#### A.6.2 Number Battled (Wild Encounters)

The Pokedex tracks how many times you have caught or defeated each species. Higher counts grant bonus shiny rerolls:

| Number Battled | Extra Shiny Rolls | Effective Odds (No Charm) | Effective Odds (With Charm) |
|----------------|------------------|--------------------------|----------------------------|
| 0 | 0 | 1/4096 | 1/1365 |
| 1-19 | +1 | 1/2048 | 1/1024 |
| 20-49 | +1 | 1/2048 | 1/1024 |
| 50-99 | +2 | 1/1365 | 1/819 |
| 100-199 | +3 | 1/1024 | 1/683 |
| 200-299 | +4 | 1/819 | 1/585 |
| 300-499 | +5 | 1/683 | 1/512 |
| 500+ | +6 | 1/585 | **1/456** |

**Important caveat:** The extra rolls from Number Battled have only a **3% chance of applying** per encounter at the 500+ threshold. The effective odds above represent the blended probability. The actual mechanism is: each encounter has a 3% chance to receive the full bonus rerolls. Most encounters still use base rates. This makes the method significantly worse than it appears at first glance.

**Why this matters for collectors:** Wild encounter shiny hunting in SwSh is the ONLY way to get shiny + mark Pokemon. Despite the deceptive Number Battled odds, this is the path to the most prestigious specimens in the game.

#### A.6.3 Max Raid Battles

- Base odds: 1/4096
- **Shiny Charm does NOT affect raid shiny odds**
- Shiny status is determined when the den spawns (beam appears), not when caught
- Promoted event raids sometimes have boosted shiny rates
- Cannot be soft-reset -- the den contents are fixed

#### A.6.4 Dynamax Adventures

- 1/300 base, **1/100 with Shiny Charm** (see Section A.4)
- Best method for shiny legendaries
- Applies to all 4 Pokemon encountered per adventure

#### A.6.5 Shiny Locks

The following Pokemon are **shiny locked** in SwSh (cannot be shiny under any circumstances):

- Zacian, Zamazenta, Eternatus (box legendaries + main story legendary)
- Galarian Articuno, Galarian Zapdos, Galarian Moltres (roaming encounters)
- Calyrex, Glastrier, Spectrier (Crown Tundra story)
- Keldeo (Crown Tundra gift)
- Galarian Slowpoke (Wedgehurst gift)
- Most in-game gift Pokemon
- Type: Null gift

**NOT shiny locked** (can be shiny):
- Kanto Articuno, Zapdos, Moltres, Mewtwo (via Dynamax Adventures)
- Regis (Crown Tundra static encounters -- soft-resettable)
- Fossil Pokemon (Dracozolt, Arctozolt, etc. -- soft-resettable)
- All wild encounters
- All bred Pokemon
- Swords of Justice (Crown Tundra static encounters)

### A.7 Breeding Mechanics Changes

#### A.7.1 Egg Move Transfer at Nursery (NEW)

Generation VIII introduced a revolutionary breeding quality-of-life change: **egg moves can be transferred between same-species Pokemon at the Nursery without breeding**.

**How it works:**
1. Place two Pokemon of the SAME species in the Nursery
2. If Pokemon A knows an egg move for that species, and Pokemon B has an empty move slot, Pokemon B will learn the egg move
3. No egg is produced -- the move is directly transferred
4. Works regardless of gender

**To create an empty move slot:** Use the Move Deleter (available at any Pokemon Center in SwSh) to delete a move.

**Collector application:** This means you can teach egg moves to any specimen after the fact -- a shiny you hatched without egg moves can receive them from a breedject. This is a massive quality-of-life improvement for competitive collectors.

#### A.7.2 Nature Mints (NEW)

Nature Mints change the **stat effects** of a Pokemon's nature without changing the nature label itself.

- Available from the Battle Tower for **50 BP each**
- Also found as overworld items in Isle of Armor and Crown Tundra
- 21 mints (one for each stat-affecting nature + 4 neutral natures)
- The actual nature (displayed in summary) remains unchanged -- only the stat multipliers change
- This means breeding still passes the original nature, Synchronize still uses the original nature, and the summary shows the original nature with a notation indicating the mint effect

**Collector significance:** Mints largely remove nature as a breeding constraint. A shiny Pokemon with a bad nature can be "fixed" with a mint for competitive play. However, purists may still value "natural" correct natures.

#### A.7.3 Ability Capsule and Ability Patch

| Item | Function | Acquisition | Cost |
|------|----------|------------|------|
| Ability Capsule | Switch between standard Ability slot 1 and slot 2 | Battle Tower | 50 BP |
| Ability Patch | Switch from standard Ability to Hidden Ability (or vice versa in Gen 9) | Dynamax Adventures (Dynite Ore) | 200 Dynite Ore |

**Ability Patch (Crown Tundra)** is a game-changer -- it allows ANY Pokemon to gain its Hidden Ability. Previously, HA was locked to specific encounter methods (SOS chains, Friend Safari, Dream Radar, etc.). Now any bred or caught specimen can receive HA after the fact.

**Collector application:** The Ability Patch removes HA as a breeding constraint. Combined with Nature Mints and egg move transfer, SwSh is the first game where a specimen can be fully "corrected" for competitive play after catch/hatch.

## Section C: Mark Hunting Guide

### C.1 Mark Hunting Fundamentals

Mark hunting requires wild encounters -- no other method produces marked Pokemon. The basic loop:

1. Set the Switch clock to the desired time period (for time marks)
2. Travel to a location with the target species during appropriate weather (for weather marks)
3. Encounter the Pokemon
4. Check the summary screen -> Ribbons tab for a mark
5. If marked: catch it. If not: flee and repeat.
6. For shiny mark hunting: also check if shiny before catching (overworld sparkles)

**Speed optimization:** Symbol encounters (overworld visible Pokemon) are faster to check because you can see the species before engaging. Running from encounters does not affect mark odds on subsequent encounters.

### C.3 Mark Probability by Weather/Time

For a wild encounter in specific conditions, the probability of any mark appearing:

| Conditions | Eligible Mark Types | Approx. Chance of ANY Mark |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Clear sky, daytime | Time + Personality + Uncommon + Rare | ~1 in 13 |
| Weather active, daytime | Time + Weather + Personality + Uncommon + Rare | ~1 in 9 |
| Weather active, nighttime | Time + Weather + Personality + Uncommon + Rare | ~1 in 9 |
| Fishing, weather active | Time + Weather + Fishing + Personality + Uncommon + Rare | ~1 in 7 |
| Fishing, clear weather | Time + Fishing + Personality + Uncommon + Rare | ~1 in 8 |

**Fishing is the best mark hunting method** due to the 1/25 Fishing Mark odds. Fish-encounter-eligible Gen 1 species (Magikarp, Goldeen, Shellder, Horsea) are the easiest marks to farm.

### C.4 Shiny + Mark Probability

The combined probability of encountering a shiny Pokemon with a mark:

| Scenario | Approximate Odds |
|----------|-----------------|
| Shiny + ANY mark (base rates) | ~1/50,000 |
| Shiny + ANY mark (500+ battled, Charm) | ~1/5,500 |
| Shiny + Rare Mark (base rates) | ~1/4,096,000 |
| Shiny + Rare Mark (500+ battled, Charm) | ~1/456,000 |
| Shiny + specific Personality Mark (base rates) | ~1/11,500,000 |

**Reality check:** Shiny + mark hunting is a marathon activity. Most collectors who achieve it spend hundreds of hours. The Number Battled shiny bonus having only a 3% chance to activate per encounter makes the effective improvement much less dramatic than the raw numbers suggest.

### E.4 Legacy Move Considerations

Several moves that were tutor-exclusive in Gen 7 (USUM) were **removed from SwSh entirely**:
- **Snatch** (Dark) -- completely removed from the game
- **Signal Beam** (Bug) -- removed
- **Telekinesis** (Psychic) -- removed

Pokemon that learned these moves in USUM retain them permanently, but these moves cannot be used or relearned in SwSh. However, they persist in the Pokemon's data and will function if the move returns in a future game.

**New in SwSh:** Some Gen 1 species gained access to moves through the IoA tutor that they never had before:
- Gengar learning **Poltergeist** (110 BP Ghost physical move) is competitively significant
- Venusaur learning **Grassy Glide** (priority in Grassy Terrain) transformed its competitive viability
- Blastoise learning **Flip Turn** gave it pivot capabilities

## Section F: Walkthrough and Transfer Strategy

### F.1 SwSh Progression for Gen 1 Collection

**Early Game (Badges 1-3):**
1. Route 1-2: Catch Caterpie, Magikarp (fishing), Oddish
2. Route 3: Vulpix, Growlithe, Machop
3. Galar Mine: Diglett, Onix
4. Route 4: Pikachu, Eevee, Galarian Meowth
5. Wild Area (first visit): Explore accessible zones, start Max Raids

**Mid Game (Badges 4-6):**
6. Motostoke Riverbank: Krabby, Snorlax (rare overworld)
7. Route 6-8: Additional encounters
8. Galar Mine No. 2: Dugtrio
9. Dusty Bowl: Machamp (overworld), Arcanine (Harsh Sunlight)
10. Wild Area expansion: More zones accessible with higher badges

**Late Game (Badges 7-8):**
11. Route 9: Shellder, Lapras
12. Route 10: Galarian Mr. Mime
13. Lake of Outrage: Ninetales, Vaporeon/Jolteon/Flareon (weather-dependent), Gyarados, Ditto

**Post-Game:**
14. Receive Charmander from Leon (Postwick) -- has Gmax factor
15. Obtain Gmax Pikachu/Eevee (if LGPE save exists)
16. Battle Tower for BP (Nature Mints, Ability Capsule, TRs)
17. Begin Shiny Charm quest (complete Galar Pokedex)

### F.2 DLC Progression

**Isle of Armor:**
1. Complete Mustard's trials to unlock Max Soup
2. Receive Bulbasaur OR Squirtle from Honey (with Gmax factor)
3. Catch IoA-exclusive Gen 1 species (Abra, Scyther, Pinsir, Tauros, etc.)
4. Farm Max Mushrooms for Max Soup (apply Gmax factor to bred/caught specimens)
5. Use Cram-o-matic for rare ball production (Apricorn Balls, Sport/Safari Ball)
6. Learn IoA tutor moves (Poltergeist for Gengar, Grassy Glide for Venusaur, etc.)

**Crown Tundra:**
7. Complete Peony's legendary clue quests
8. Encounter Galarian legendary birds (chase across Galar -- shiny locked)
9. Begin Dynamax Adventures for shiny legendary hunting
10. Catch Crown Tundra-exclusive Gen 1 species (Nidoran lines, Zubat, Jynx, Electabuzz, Magmar, fossil Pokemon)
11. Farm Dynite Ore for Ability Patches
12. Teach Crown Tundra tutor moves

### F.3 Wild Area Exploration Strategy

**Weather rotation approach:**
- Check Wild Area weather daily (changes at midnight)
- Prioritize Fog weather (Champion-only) for rare Psychic/Ghost/Fairy spawns
- Thunderstorm for Electabuzz-family spawns
- Harsh Sunlight for Fire-type Gmax raids

**Max Raid farming:**
- Check dens daily (reset at midnight)
- Purple beams indicate rare dens (Gmax potential)
- Use Wishing Pieces to force den spawns (purchased from Watt Traders)
- Host raids for better catch rates

### F.4 Dynamax Adventure Strategy for Shiny Legendaries

1. **Get the Shiny Charm first** -- reduces average hunts from ~300 to ~100
2. **Save before entering** (the legendary in the final room is random until you discover all available legendaries via Peonia's hints)
3. **Never keep a non-shiny legendary** -- this removes it from your pool permanently
4. **Ball selection**: Bring your preferred rare balls. The catch is guaranteed, so use the most aesthetically pleasing option
5. **Speed strategy**: Solo with NPCs is faster per run than waiting for online matchmaking, though success rate is lower
6. **Track your count**: Average ~100 adventures per shiny with Charm. Budget 25-40 hours per target
7. **Multi-target runs**: While hunting one legendary, the 3 non-legendary Pokemon in each adventure also have 1/100 (Charm) shiny odds. Keep any shinies you find along the way

### F.5 Mark Hunting Approach

1. **Choose your target species** -- must be available as a wild encounter
2. **Select optimal location** -- highest spawn rate for the target
3. **Set weather/time** -- for desired mark type (or any conditions for maximum mark variety)
4. **Encounter loop**: See Pokemon -> encounter -> check marks -> catch or flee -> repeat
5. **For shiny marks**: Same loop but also watching for shiny sparkles in the overworld before engaging
6. **Fishing marks**: If your target is a fishing encounter species, this is the fastest method due to 1/25 Fishing Mark odds

### F.6 Pre-Transfer Checklist

Complete these activities before sending specimens to HOME or newer games:

- [ ] All desired Gigantamax factors applied via Max Soup (Gmax factor has no effect outside SwSh)
- [ ] Galar Champion Ribbon earned (story completion with specimen in party)
- [ ] Tower Master Ribbon earned (defeat Leon at Battle Tower top)
- [ ] Master Rank Ribbon earned (win at Master Ball tier in ranked online -- optional, very difficult)
- [ ] Effort Ribbon obtained (max EVs, show to Hammerlocke NPC)
- [ ] Best Friends Ribbon obtained (max friendship, show to Hammerlocke NPC)
- [ ] All desired marks acquired (check mark hunting targets before leaving the game)
- [ ] IoA tutor moves taught (Poltergeist, Grassy Glide, etc.)
- [ ] Crown Tundra tutor moves taught
- [ ] Nature Mints applied if needed (effect persists through transfer)
- [ ] Ability Patch used if HA is desired (persists through transfer)
- [ ] Dynamax Adventure shiny hunts completed (1/100 odds do not exist elsewhere)

**Transfer note:** Pokemon transferred from SwSh to HOME can go to Scarlet/Violet (if compatible) or other games but **cannot return to SwSh**. The Gmax factor, marks, and ribbons all persist in HOME and newer games, but Gmax specifically only functions in SwSh.

## Section G: Summary -- Gen 8 Collector Priorities

### G.1 What Makes Gen 8 Unique

1. **Marks** -- the first randomized cosmetic collector attribute; creates an entirely new prestige axis (shiny + mark)
2. **Gigantamax forms** -- 12 Gen 1 species have unique Gmax transformations exclusive to SwSh
3. **Dynamax Adventures** -- 1/100 shiny odds for legendary Pokemon (best in series)
4. **Galarian forms** -- 11 Gen 1 species with regional variants, plus 3 unique Galarian evolutions (Perrserker, Sirfetch'd, Mr. Rime)
5. **Galarian legendary birds** -- mark-eligible legendary Pokemon (unique collector opportunity)
6. **Ball diversity** -- Sport Ball, Safari Ball, and Apricorn Balls all available for catching via Cram-o-matic
7. **Ability Patch** -- Hidden Ability remediation for any specimen
8. **Nature Mints** -- stat-effect correction without changing actual nature
9. **Egg move transfer** -- same-species Nursery move sharing without breeding
10. **Dexit** -- not all Gen 1 species are available; ~36 are completely excluded

### G.3 Critical Warnings

1. **Dexit is real.** Verify your target species exists in SwSh before planning. ~36 Gen 1 species are completely excluded.
2. **Dynamax Adventure legendary catches are one-per-save.** Never keep a non-shiny if you want the shiny. Once kept, it is gone from your pool forever.
3. **Galarian legendary birds are shiny locked.** Do not waste time soft-resetting for shiny Galarian Articuno/Zapdos/Moltres.
4. **Marks only come from wild encounters.** Raids, eggs, gifts, and trades cannot have marks.
5. **Gmax factor only functions in SwSh.** It persists in data but has no effect in HOME or newer games.
6. **The Number Battled shiny method is deceptive.** The bonus rerolls only have a 3% activation chance per encounter. Actual effective odds at 500+ battles are ~1/456 with Charm, not the 1/455 that raw numbers might suggest.
7. **Max Raid shiny odds are NOT affected by Shiny Charm.** Only Dynamax Adventures benefit from the Charm for raid-type content.
8. **Gift Gmax Pikachu/Eevee/Meowth cannot evolve** while they have the Gmax factor. Use Max Soup to remove the factor before evolving.
9. **Kanto Meowth is rare in Galar.** Galarian Meowth is the wild form. Kanto Meowth requires in-game trade, transfer, or the expired Gmax event. Obtain via breeding after acquiring one.
10. **Transfer out of SwSh is one-way.** Once a Pokemon leaves SwSh for HOME and enters another game, it cannot return to SwSh. Complete all SwSh-specific activities first.
