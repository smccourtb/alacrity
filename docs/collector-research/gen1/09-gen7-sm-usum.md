# Gen 7 (SM + USUM) Collector Research

Generation VII is a **pivotal generation for Gen 1 collectors** -- Pokemon Sun/Moon (2016) and Ultra Sun/Ultra Moon (2017) introduced **Alolan forms** (the first regional variants), **Beast Balls** (a prestige-tier collectible ball), **Hyper Training** (IV remediation for imperfect specimens), **SOS Battles** (the primary HA and shiny hunting method), and crucially, SM/USUM are the **only games that accept Virtual Console transfers from Pokemon Bank**. This is where Game Boy origin specimens enter the modern ecosystem.

**The headline features are Alolan forms and VC transfer reception.** Eighteen Gen 1 Pokemon received Alolan forms -- entirely new typings, abilities, and designs that create a whole new axis of collector interest. Every Alolan form specimen is a distinct target from its Kanto counterpart, effectively doubling the collection scope for those species.

**USUM is strictly superior to SM for collectors.** USUM has BP move tutors (SM has none), Ultra Wormhole legendary encounters (including Mewtwo and the Kanto birds), the Totem-sized Alolan Marowak gift, and a larger Alola Pokedex. SM has no exclusive collector advantages.

For Gen 6 mechanics (Mega Evolution, Bank infrastructure, Destiny Knot breeding, origin marks), see [08-gen6-xy-oras.md](08-gen6-xy-oras.md). This document assumes familiarity with those systems and focuses on what Gen 7 adds or changes.

## Section A: Game-Wide Mechanics

### A.1 Alolan Forms (NEW -- CRITICAL)

Alolan forms are regional variants of existing Pokemon, introduced in Generation VII. Pokemon that migrated to the tropical Alola region adapted to its unique island ecosystems, developing different types, abilities, and appearances. These are the first regional forms in the series (Galarian forms follow in Gen 8, Hisuian in Legends: Arceus, Paldean in Gen 9).

**Collector significance:** Each Alolan form is a distinct collectible specimen from its Kanto counterpart. A complete Gen 1 collection now requires BOTH Kanto and Alolan forms for all 18 species. Alolan forms also open prestige specimen targets: Beast Ball Alolan forms, Game Boy origin Alolan forms (via VC transfer + evolution), shiny Alolan forms, and HA Alolan forms.

#### A.1.2 Evolution Mechanics in Alola

**Default behavior:** Any Pokemon that evolves in Alola into a species with an Alolan form will ALWAYS produce the Alolan form, regardless of the parent's origin. This includes:
- Pikachu evolved via Thunder Stone in Alola -> Alolan Raichu (not Kanto Raichu)
- Exeggcute evolved via Leaf Stone in Alola -> Alolan Exeggutor (not Kanto Exeggutor)
- Cubone evolved at level 28+ at night in Alola -> Alolan Marowak (not Kanto Marowak)

**This applies even to transferred Pokemon.** A Pikachu transferred from Gen 6, VC Gen 1, or any other origin will evolve into Alolan Raichu when evolved in SM/USUM. There is no way to obtain Kanto Raichu, Kanto Exeggutor, or Kanto Marowak by evolving in SM/USUM.

**To get Kanto forms of evolution-only species in SM/USUM:** You must transfer the already-evolved Kanto form from another game (Gen 6 via Bank, or trade).

#### A.1.3 Breeding and the Everstone Form Trick

Breeding in Alola defaults to Alolan offspring for species with Alolan forms. However, the Everstone can override this:

- **Kanto-form parent holding Everstone** -> offspring is Kanto form (even when bred in Alola)
- **Alolan-form parent holding Everstone** -> offspring is Alolan form (the default anyway)
- **Both parents hold Everstone** -> offspring matches the female parent's form
- **No Everstone** -> offspring always matches the region (Alolan form in Alola)

**Collector application:** To breed Kanto-form specimens in SM/USUM, transfer a Kanto-form parent from another game, give it an Everstone, and breed. The offspring will be Kanto form with the Alola Clover mark -- a unique combination.

**Critical limitation:** Even with the Everstone trick, you can breed Kanto Pikachu/Exeggcute/Cubone in Alola, but evolving them in Alola still produces the Alolan form. To get Kanto Raichu/Exeggutor/Marowak, you must evolve them in a non-Alola game.

#### A.1.4 Game Boy Origin Alolan Forms

This is a prestige specimen class unique to Gen 7:

1. Catch a Pikachu, Exeggcute, or Cubone in VC Red/Blue/Yellow
2. Control its Nature via EXP mod 25 before transfer
3. Transfer through Poke Transporter -> Bank -> withdraw in SM/USUM
4. The specimen arrives with the **Game Boy origin mark**, Hidden Ability, and 3 guaranteed perfect IVs
5. Evolve in Alola: Pikachu -> **Alolan Raichu**, Exeggcute -> **Alolan Exeggutor**, Cubone -> **Alolan Marowak**

The result is an Alolan form bearing the Game Boy origin mark -- proof of Game Boy ancestry combined with an Alolan regional form. This is only possible for the three evolution-triggered Alolan forms (Raichu, Exeggutor, Marowak), since Rattata/Sandshrew/Vulpix/Diglett/Meowth/Geodude/Grimer already exist as base forms with Alolan variants obtainable wild.

**Only SM/USUM can receive VC transfers.** This makes Gen 7 the sole gateway for creating Game Boy origin Alolan specimens.

### A.2 VC Transfer Destination (CRITICAL JUNCTION)

SM/USUM are the **only games** that accept Pokemon transferred from Virtual Console releases of Gen 1 (RBY) and Gen 2 (GSC) via Pokemon Bank. This is the critical junction where Game Boy-era specimens enter the modern ecosystem.

#### A.2.1 Transfer Process

```
VC Game (3DS) -> Poke Transporter -> Pokemon Bank -> Withdraw in SM/USUM
```

Pokemon Bank can hold VC-transferred Pokemon indefinitely, but they can ONLY be withdrawn to SM/USUM -- not to Gen 6 games, not to later games directly. After withdrawal to SM/USUM, the Pokemon can then be sent back to Bank and onward to HOME.

#### A.2.2 What Happens During Transfer

| Attribute | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **Nature** | Determined by original EXP mod 25 (set before transfer) |
| **IVs** | Randomly regenerated; 3 guaranteed to be 31 (5 for Mew/Celebi) |
| **Ability** | **Hidden Ability** assigned to all VC transfers |
| **Origin Mark** | **Game Boy mark** (distinct from Clover/Pentagon/etc.) |
| **Poke Ball** | Standard Poke Ball (the only ball in Gen 1/2) |
| **OT/ID** | Preserved from VC save |
| **Moves** | Preserved but may become invalid if not in the SM/USUM move list |
| **Level** | Preserved |
| **Shiny status** | Determined by DVs in Gen 2 (possible); Gen 1 uses Gen 2 DV-to-shiny conversion |

#### A.2.3 Reception Strategy

After withdrawing VC Pokemon in SM/USUM, you should:

1. **Check Nature** -- confirm the EXP mod 25 worked as intended
2. **Evaluate IVs** -- use the Judge function (unlocked at Battle Tree after hatching 20 eggs)
3. **Hyper Train** if needed -- Level 100 + Bottle Caps can fix imperfect IVs
4. **Teach USUM tutor moves** -- this is the LAST 3DS-era tutor set (SM has no tutors)
5. **Complete ribbons** -- Alola Champion, Battle Tree, Best Friends, Effort, Footprint
6. **Evolve if desired** -- Alolan evolution is permanent; Kanto evolution requires sending to a non-Alola game first
7. **Send to Bank** -- then to HOME when ready (one-way Bank -> HOME)

**Warning:** Do NOT send VC specimens to HOME before completing all Gen 7 activities. Bank -> HOME is one-way, and SM/USUM-exclusive tutors/ribbons/evolutions become permanently unavailable.

#### A.2.4 Special Mew Rules

Only Mew with OT "GF" (or Japanese "Game Freak") and Trainer ID 22796 will pass Poke Transporter's legality check -- specifically the Mew distributed for VC Pokemon Red/Blue/Yellow. Random Mew from glitches will not transfer.

### A.3 Beast Balls (NEW -- PRESTIGE)

The Beast Ball is a new Poke Ball type introduced in Gen 7 with an extremely distinctive appearance (a blue/white high-tech design with yellow accents). Originally designed for catching Ultra Beasts, it has a dramatically different catch rate on regular Pokemon.

#### A.3.1 Catch Rate

| Target | Catch Rate Modifier |
|--------|-------------------|
| Ultra Beasts | 5x |
| Non-Ultra Beasts | **0.1x** (~410/4096) |

The 0.1x multiplier makes Beast Ball catches on normal Pokemon extremely difficult -- roughly a 90% reduction in catch chance compared to a standard Poke Ball. This extreme difficulty is exactly what gives Beast Ball specimens their prestige value.

#### A.3.2 Obtaining Beast Balls

**Sun/Moon:**
- Provided during Ultra Beast missions (Looker/Anabel questline, post-game)
- Auto-replenished if you run out during UB missions
- Routes 2, 8, 13; Seafolk Village; Aether Paradise (limited supply)

**Ultra Sun/Ultra Moon:**
- Altar of the Sunne/Moone; Poni Grove (limited)
- Aether Paradise (repeatable source)
- More accessible due to Ultra Wormhole mechanics

#### A.3.3 Breeding Inheritance

Beast Balls **can be passed down through breeding** -- they follow the same inheritance rules as other Poke Balls:
- Female parent's ball passes to offspring (when breeding with a male of different species)
- Non-Ditto parent's ball passes when breeding with Ditto
- Same-species breeding: 50/50 chance of either parent's ball

This means a single Beast Ball catch can produce unlimited Beast Ball offspring through breeding. This is the key enabler for Beast Ball collecting -- you only need to make the painful 0.1x catch once.

**Non-inheritable balls:** Master Ball, Cherish Ball, and Strange Ball cannot be passed down (offspring gets standard Poke Ball instead).

#### A.3.4 Beast Ball Collector Value

Beast Ball specimens are considered prestige-tier for collectors because:
1. The 0.1x catch rate makes each catch a significant time investment
2. The distinctive blue/yellow ball design is visually striking
3. Beast Balls are limited in quantity (without breeding)
4. A Beast Ball Alolan form is a premium dual-rarity specimen
5. Beast Ball + HA + shiny is the ultimate prestige target

### A.4 Hyper Training (NEW)

Hyper Training allows Level 100 Pokemon to have their effective IVs boosted to 31 ("Best") using Bottle Caps. This was introduced in Gen 7 and is a game-changer for specimens with imperfect IVs.

#### A.4.1 Mechanics

| Item | Effect | Requirement |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Bottle Cap** | Boosts one IV stat to 31 equivalent | Pokemon must be Level 100 |
| **Gold Bottle Cap** | Boosts ALL six IV stats to 31 equivalent | Pokemon must be Level 100 |

**Location:** Mr. Hyper in Hau'oli City Shopping Mall (Melemele Island). Requires Champion status.

**Critical detail:** Hyper Training does NOT change the Pokemon's actual IV value -- it applies a game-level override. This means:
- **Breeding** still uses the real (unmodified) IV
- **Hidden Power type** is still based on the real IVs
- The hyper-trained stat displays as "Hyper Trained!" rather than "Best"

#### A.4.2 Collector Significance

Hyper Training makes imperfect IV specimens competitively viable without breeding. This is especially important for:
- **VC transfers** (random IVs, only 3 guaranteed 31s)
- **Legendary Pokemon** (cannot breed)
- **Event Pokemon** (fixed distributions)
- **Shiny Pokemon** (may have imperfect IVs from hunting)

A VC Mewtwo with only 3 perfect IVs can be Hyper Trained to function as a 6-IV specimen in battle.

#### A.4.3 Obtaining Bottle Caps

| Method | Location | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Found item | Paniola Town, Poni Gauntlet (USUM) | One-time pickups |
| Fishing | Poni Island (Seafolk Village, Poni Wilds, Poni Breaker Coast, Poni Gauntlet, Poni Meadow) | Rare fishing drops |
| Festival Plaza | Lottery shops (Treasure Hunt 2nd prize), Haunted Houses, Castle trades (30 same-color Shards) | Repeatable |
| Battle Tree | 30 consecutive victories reward | Repeatable |
| Magearna event | Hau'oli City QR code Magearna holds one | One-time |

### A.5 SOS Battles (NEW -- MAJOR HUNTING METHOD)

In Gen 7, wild Pokemon can call for help during battle, summoning allies. This creates "SOS chains" where consecutive ally calls improve the quality of appearing Pokemon.

#### A.5.1 Chain Bonuses

| Chain Length | Shiny Rolls | HA Chance | Guaranteed Perfect IVs |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|----------------------|
| 0-10 | 1 (base) | 0% | 0 |
| 11-20 | 5 | 5% | 1 |
| 21-30 | 9 | 10% | 2 |
| 31+ | 13 | **15%** | **4** |

**SM vs USUM difference:** In SM, the chain counter resets to 0 after 255 calls, losing all bonuses. In USUM, the counter stops at 255 but bonuses persist indefinitely. USUM is strictly better for SOS chaining.

#### A.5.2 SOS Battle Shiny Odds

| Chain Length | No Charm | With Shiny Charm |
|-------------|----------|-----------------|
| 0-10 | 1/4,096 | 1/1,365 |
| 11-20 | 1/820 | 1/586 |
| 21-30 | 1/456 | 1/373 |
| **31+** | **1/316** | **1/274** |

SOS chaining at 31+ is the best wild encounter shiny method in Gen 7, surpassing even the Masuda Method without a Charm.

**Shiny Charm:** Obtained after completing the Alola Pokedex. In SM, this requires ~302 Pokemon; in USUM, ~403 Pokemon.

### A.7 Breeding -- Gen 7 Changes

Gen 7 made several important changes to breeding mechanics from Gen 6.

#### A.7.1 Ball Inheritance (UPDATED)

| Breeding Scenario | Gen 6 Rule | Gen 7 Rule |
|-------------------|-----------|-----------|
| Male + Female (different species) | Female's ball | Female's ball |
| Male + Female (same species) | Female's ball | **50/50 either parent's ball** |
| Male + Ditto | Poke Ball (ignored) | **Male's ball** |
| Female + Ditto | Female's ball | Female's ball |
| Genderless + Ditto | Poke Ball | **Non-Ditto parent's ball** |

**Key Gen 7 changes:**
- Same-species breeding now has a 50/50 chance of inheriting either parent's ball
- Males can pass their ball when breeding with Ditto (impossible in Gen 6)
- Genderless Pokemon can pass their ball when breeding with Ditto

**Non-inheritable:** Master Ball, Cherish Ball, and Strange Ball always default to standard Poke Ball.

#### A.7.2 HA Passing

- 60% chance from either gender parent when breeding with Ditto (unchanged from Gen 6)
- 60% chance from female parent in male + female breeding (unchanged)
- Males still cannot pass HA in male + female breeding (must use Ditto or female HA parent)

#### A.7.3 Egg Moves

Unchanged from Gen 6 -- both parents can pass egg moves. The Pokemon Nursery (Paniola Ranch on Akala Island) replaces the Day Care but functions identically for breeding.

#### A.7.4 Regional Form Breeding

See Section A.1.3 above for Everstone form control. Default breeding in Alola always produces Alolan offspring for species with Alolan forms.

### A.11 Apricorn Balls in Gen 7

In SM/USUM, Apricorn Balls from HGSS are available as items for the first time since Gen 4.

**How to obtain:** In the GAME FREAK building in Heahea City (Akala Island), speak to a specific NPC while you have a Pokemon transferred from VC Gold/Silver in your party. You receive one set of Apricorn Balls:
- Fast Ball, Friend Ball, Level Ball, Lure Ball, Heavy Ball, Love Ball, Moon Ball

**One set per game file.** These are extremely limited -- 1 of each ball per save. They can be passed down through breeding (same rules as other balls), so a single catch can propagate the ball type.

**Collector note:** Apricorn Ball + Alolan form is a premium combination. A Moon Ball Alolan Vulpix or Heavy Ball Alolan Geodude are aesthetically matched prestige specimens.

### D.3 Breeding Beast Ball Specimens

Once you have ONE Beast Ball specimen, breeding propagates the ball:
1. Catch target in Beast Ball (the painful part)
2. Breed with Ditto -> offspring inherits Beast Ball
3. All subsequent offspring carry Beast Ball
4. Same-species breeding: 50/50 for either parent's ball

This means a single Beast Ball Eevee can produce Beast Ball Vaporeon, Jolteon, Flareon, Espeon, Umbreon, Leafeon, Glaceon, and Sylveon through breeding + evolution. The investment pays dividends across all Eeveelutions.

1. **Alolan forms** -- 18 new specimens for Gen 1 collectors, each a distinct target
