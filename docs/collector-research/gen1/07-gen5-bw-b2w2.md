# Gen 5 (BW + B2W2) Collector Research

Generation V is a **landmark generation for collectors** -- Black/White (2010-2011) and Black 2/White 2 (2012) introduced **Hidden Abilities** (the third ability slot), the **Dream World** online service, **Hidden Grottos**, the **Shiny Charm**, and fundamental breeding improvements. Gen 5 is also the **last generation before Pokemon Bank** -- the transition from local DS-to-DS transfers (Poke Transfer) to cloud-based storage (Poke Transporter -> Bank) happens at the Gen 5/Gen 6 boundary.

**The headline feature is Hidden Abilities.** Every Pokemon species received a third ability slot in Gen 5, but obtaining a specimen with that ability required specific methods -- many tied to the now-defunct Dream World online service. For Gen 1 species, some Hidden Abilities were only ever released through Dream World, making them **historically obtainable in Gen 5 but requiring Gen 6+ methods today**.

Gen 5 also introduced **Dream Balls** (a unique Poke Ball from the Entree Forest and Dream Radar), made TMs **infinitely reusable** for the first time, and brought the **Masuda Method + Shiny Charm** combination that dramatically improved shiny breeding odds.

For Gen 4 mechanics (Apricorn Balls, Masuda Method introduction, physical/special split), see [06-gen4-dppt-hgss.md](06-gen4-dppt-hgss.md). This document assumes familiarity with those systems and focuses on what Gen 5 adds or changes.

## Section A: Game-Wide Mechanics

### A.1 Hidden Abilities (NEW -- CRITICAL)

Hidden Abilities are the **single most important mechanical addition** in Gen 5 for collectors. Every Pokemon species was assigned a third ability slot (the "Hidden Ability" or HA). Unlike regular abilities (Ability 1 and Ability 2), Hidden Abilities cannot be encountered through normal wild battles -- they require specific sources.

#### A.1.1 Gen 5 Hidden Ability Sources

| Source | Games | Method | Ball | Status |
|--------|-------|--------|------|--------|
| **Dream World** | BW, B2W2 | Online browser game; befriend Pokemon in Entree Forest | **Dream Ball** (or player's choice) | **DEFUNCT** (shut down Jan 14, 2014) |
| **Hidden Grotto** | B2W2 only | Overworld locations; 5% regeneration chance per 256 steps | Standard wild ball | **Still functional** (offline feature) |
| **Dream Radar** | B2W2 only | 3DS eShop app; AR shooting game | **Dream Ball** | **Partially available** (3DS eShop closed March 2023; works if already downloaded) |
| **Events** | BW, B2W2 | Wi-Fi/serial distributions | Cherish Ball or Dream Ball | **DEFUNCT** (Wi-Fi services terminated) |
| **NPC Gifts/Trades** | B2W2 | In-game NPCs giving HA Pokemon | Varies | **Still functional** |

**Dream World shutdown impact:** The Dream World was the primary HA source for the vast majority of Gen 1 species in Gen 5. Its closure means most Gen 1 HAs from Gen 5 are only obtainable today through **Hidden Grottos** (limited selection) or by using **Gen 6+ methods** (Friend Safari, DexNav, SOS Battles, Max Raids, Ability Patch, etc.).

#### A.1.2 Hidden Ability Inheritance (Breeding)

Hidden Abilities can be bred onto offspring, but with restrictions that differ between BW and B2W2:

**Black/White:**
- Only the **female** parent's ability is relevant when breeding male + female
- If the female has her HA, there is a **60% chance** the offspring inherits it
- Breeding HA female + Ditto does **NOT** pass the HA in BW
- Males cannot pass their HA at all in BW

**Black 2/White 2:**
- Female HA + male breeding: **80% chance** of HA passing (improved from 60%)
- Breeding HA female + Ditto still does **NOT** pass the HA in B2W2
- Males still cannot pass HA in Gen 5

**Collector implication:** In Gen 5, you need a **female** HA specimen to breed HA offspring. For species with skewed gender ratios (87.5% male starters, genderless species), this made HA breeding extremely difficult or impossible without later-generation mechanics. Starting in Gen 6, males and genderless Pokemon can pass HA when bred with Ditto.

### A.2 Dream Ball (NEW)

The Dream Ball is a unique Poke Ball introduced in Gen 5. It has a distinctive pink/purple appearance and was obtained exclusively through two defunct or limited sources:

**Entree Forest (Dream World):**
- When a Pokemon was befriended in the Dream World and sent to the Entree Forest, the player could catch it in a special encounter
- A single Dream Ball appeared in the player's Bag for this encounter (in addition to regular balls)
- Any Poke Ball used in the Entree Forest caught without fail -- the Dream Ball was cosmetic
- The player could choose to use a regular ball instead, but the Dream Ball was the default

**Dream Radar (B2W2 3DS App):**
- All Pokemon transferred from Dream Radar to B2W2 arrived in Dream Balls automatically
- This includes Slowpoke, Staryu, Porygon, and legendaries (Tornadus, Thundurus, Landorus, plus Gen 4 legendaries with cartridges inserted)

**Current status:**
- Dream World is **defunct** -- no new Dream Ball Pokemon can be obtained this way
- Dream Radar is **partially available** (works if already downloaded before 3DS eShop closure)
- Starting in Gen 8 (Sword/Shield), Dream Balls became obtainable as rare items and function as catch balls with 4x rate on sleeping Pokemon
- Historical Dream Ball specimens from Gen 5 are **legitimate and transferable** through Bank -> HOME

**Gen 1 species obtainable in Dream Balls in Gen 5:**
- Any Gen 1 species available in the Dream World could be caught in a Dream Ball
- Slowpoke, Staryu, and Porygon from Dream Radar always come in Dream Balls
- These are the earliest possible Dream Ball specimens for these species

### A.3 Egg-of-Origin Change (NEW)

Starting in Gen 5, a hatched Pokemon's **game of origin** is determined by the game in which it **hatched**, not the game where the egg was produced. This means:

- If you produce an egg in Pokemon White and trade it to Pokemon Black 2 before hatching, the hatched Pokemon has a B2W2 origin mark
- This change persists in all subsequent generations
- For collectors tracking origin marks, this means eggs should be hatched in the "correct" game if origin matters

### A.4 Breeding Mechanics

#### A.4.1 Everstone Nature Passing

| Game | Mechanic | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Black/White** | **50% chance** when either parent holds Everstone | Same rate as Gen 4, BUT now works with Masuda Method (fixed the Gen 4 conflict!) |
| **Black 2/White 2** | **100% guarantee** when either parent holds Everstone | Major improvement -- nature control is now deterministic |

**Critical improvement over Gen 4:** In Gen 4, using Masuda Method (foreign parents) disabled Everstone nature passing due to both being tied to the personality value. In Gen 5, nature is **independent of the personality value**, so Everstone + Masuda Method work simultaneously. This is a massive quality-of-life improvement for shiny breeding.

#### A.4.2 Ability Inheritance

Gen 5 introduces ability inheritance for the first time:

**Non-Hidden Abilities:**
- When breeding female + male, the offspring has an **80% chance** (B2W2) of inheriting the female's ability slot
- This means you can now influence which of the two regular abilities the offspring gets

**Hidden Abilities:**
- Female with HA + male: **60%** chance in BW, **80%** in B2W2
- Female with HA + Ditto: Does NOT pass HA in Gen 5 (fixed in Gen 6)
- Male with HA + Ditto: Does NOT pass HA in Gen 5 (fixed in Gen 6)
- Male with HA + female: Does NOT pass HA

**Genderless species with HA** (Magnemite, Voltorb, Staryu, Porygon): Cannot breed HA offspring in Gen 5 at all, since they can only breed with Ditto and Ditto breeding doesn't pass HA. This was fixed in Gen 6.

#### A.4.3 IV Inheritance

Same as Gen 4: 3 random IVs inherited from random parents. Power Items still guarantee one specific stat. No changes.

#### A.4.4 Masuda Method

| Game | Base Shiny Rate | Masuda Rate | Shiny Charm | Masuda + Charm |
|------|----------------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| BW | 1/8192 | ~1/1366 (6/8192) | N/A (no Charm) | N/A |
| B2W2 | 1/8192 | ~1/1366 (6/8192) | ~1/2731 (3/8192) | **~1/1024 (8/8192)** |

The Gen 5 Masuda Method generates **5 additional personality values**, making shiny odds approximately 6x the base rate.

#### A.4.5 Shiny Charm (NEW -- B2W2 Only)

The Shiny Charm is a **Key Item** introduced in B2W2, awarded by Professor Juniper for **completing the National Pokedex** (all 649 Pokemon seen/caught). It generates **2 additional rerolls** for shininess on every encounter and every egg.

- Wild encounters: ~1/2731 (3/8192)
- Masuda Method + Shiny Charm: **~1/1024 (8/8192)** -- the best pre-Gen 6 shiny odds
- The Charm works passively while in the Bag

**Collector note:** Completing the National Dex in B2W2 requires extensive trading and transferring. However, the payoff for shiny hunting is enormous, especially combined with Masuda Method.

#### A.4.6 Daycare and Ditto

**Daycare locations:**
- BW: Route 3 (accessible early)
- B2W2: Route 3 (accessible post-Elite Four)

**Ditto locations:**
- BW: Giant Chasm (post-game)
- B2W2: Giant Chasm (post-game), Hidden Grotto in Giant Chasm (HA Imposter Ditto)

**Imposter Ditto** from the Hidden Grotto is notable for competitive use but does not affect breeding differently from a normal Ditto.

### A.5 Transfer Mechanics

Gen 5 sits at a critical juncture in the transfer chain -- it receives from Gen 4 via a DS-to-DS method and sends forward to Bank via a 3DS application.

#### A.5.1 Gen 4 -> Gen 5: Poke Transfer

- **Location:** Poke Transfer Lab on Route 15 (BW/B2W2), post-National Dex
- **Method:** DS Download Play between two DS-family systems. Gen 4 game in one system, Gen 5 game in the other.
- **Limit:** 6 Pokemon at a time via a catching minigame (bow-and-ball game with time limit)
- **Restrictions:**
  - Pokemon cannot know HM moves (Surf, Fly, Waterfall, etc.)
  - **Items are NOT transferred** -- they are stripped. Remove valuable items first!
  - Eggs cannot be transferred
  - Spiky-eared Pichu cannot be transferred
- **Ball preservation:** All Poke Ball types preserved, including HGSS Apricorn Balls
- **Hardware:** Two DS-family systems required (DS, DS Lite, DSi, 3DS all work)
- **One-way:** Pokemon cannot return to Gen 4

**CRITICAL: Remove items before transferring.** Unlike Gen 3->4 (Pal Park), Poke Transfer strips held items. Lucky Eggs, Leftovers, Master Balls, and other valuables will be lost.

**CRITICAL: Forget HM moves before transferring.** Any HM move blocks the transfer entirely.

#### A.5.2 Gen 5 -> Bank: Poke Transporter

- **Method:** 3DS application (Poke Transporter) reads Gen 5 cartridge and sends Box 1 contents to Pokemon Bank's Transport Box
- **Limit:** Entire Box 1 (up to 30 Pokemon at once) -- much faster than Poke Transfer
- **Restrictions:**
  - Held items are returned to the Gen 5 game's Bag (or deleted if Bag is full)
  - Cannot transfer if Transport Box in Bank already has Pokemon
- **Ball preservation:** All ball types preserved, including Dream Balls
- **One-way:** Pokemon cannot return to Gen 5
- **Destination:** Transport Box in Pokemon Bank. From there, withdraw to Gen 6 (XY/ORAS), Gen 7 (SM/USUM), or move to Pokemon HOME

**Poke Transporter also handles VC Gen 1/2 transfers** -- the same app transfers from Virtual Console Red/Blue/Yellow/Gold/Silver/Crystal to Bank.

#### A.5.3 Complete Transfer Chain

```
Gen 4 (DPPt/HGSS)
    |
    v  [Poke Transfer -- 6 at a time, DS Download Play, no items]
Gen 5 (BW/B2W2)
    |
    v  [Poke Transporter -- Box 1, up to 30, 3DS app]
Pokemon Bank (3DS)
    |
    v  [Bank -> HOME, or withdraw to Gen 6/7]
Pokemon HOME / Gen 6 / Gen 7 / Gen 8 / Gen 9
```

### A.6 Ribbons

**Generation V is the only generation since ribbons were introduced (Gen 3) with zero in-game ribbon opportunities.** (Gen 1 and Gen 2 predate ribbons entirely.)

There are no contests, no battle facility ribbons, no NPC-given ribbons, and no purchasable ribbons in any Gen 5 game. The only ribbons that exist on Gen 5 Pokemon are:

- Ribbons transferred from Gen 3/4 (retained through Poke Transfer)
- Ribbons pre-attached to event distributions (Cherish Ball event Pokemon)

**Collector implication:** There is nothing ribbon-related to accomplish in Gen 5. If you want ribbons on a specimen that will pass through Gen 5, earn them in Gen 4 first, then transfer.

### A.7 TMs -- Now Infinitely Reusable (NEW)

Starting in Gen 5, TMs are **no longer consumed on use**. Once obtained, a TM can be taught to as many Pokemon as desired. This is a massive quality-of-life change:

- No need to hoard TMs or save them for specific Pokemon
- Every TM can be taught to every compatible Pokemon in the party/PC
- TMs are now treated like Key Items in terms of reusability

Gen 5 expanded the TM list to **TM01-TM95** plus **HM01-HM06**.

5. **Dream Radar:** If you have the 3DS app, capture Slowpoke, Staryu, Porygon with HA in Dream Balls

#### E.1.2 BW Considerations

- No Gen 1 species available until post-National Dex
- White version has White Forest with 11 Gen 1 species (Pidgey, Nidoran-F/M, Oddish, Abra, Machop, Bellsprout, Magnemite, Gastly, Rhyhorn, Porygon)
- Black version has no White Forest equivalent for Gen 1 species
- Fewer wild Gen 1 options compared to B2W2

### E.2 When to Use Poke Transporter

Transfer Gen 5 specimens to Bank when:
- All desired tutor moves have been learned (B2W2 tutors are the last pre-Bank set)
- All breeding is complete (HA inheritance works differently in Gen 5 vs Gen 6+)
- Any Gen 5-specific TM moves are taught
- You're satisfied with the specimen's nature, IVs, and ability

**Before transferring, ensure:**
1. All HM moves are forgotten (Move Deleter in Mistralton City for BW, PWT area for B2W2)
2. Valuable held items are removed (items go back to Bag, or are deleted if Bag is full)
3. Arrange Pokemon in Box 1 (Poke Transporter takes entire Box 1)

### E.3 What to Finish Before Transferring

| Priority | Task | Why |

| **HIGH** | Learn B2W2 tutor moves | Cannot return to learn them later |
| **HIGH** | Breed HA specimens from Hidden Grottos | Need female HA for Gen 5 breeding; easier to breed HA in Gen 6+ but specimens stay in Gen 5 |

| MEDIUM | Farm Imposter Ditto from Giant Chasm | Useful for competitive; transferable |

### E.4 Hidden Grotto Farming Strategy

For HA specimens from Hidden Grottos:

1. **Target one grotto at a time** -- save in front of it, check contents
2. **If empty or wrong Pokemon:** Walk 256+ steps (use Bicycle), return and check again
3. **Regeneration rate:** 5% chance per 256 steps = ~5,120 steps per expected regeneration
4. **Contents are random:** Pokemon, hidden item, or nothing. Only ~40% chance of a Pokemon appearing
5. **Each grotto has a pool of 3-4 Pokemon** -- you need the right species AND it has a fixed encounter rate within that pool
6. **Realistic time investment:** Getting a specific HA Pokemon from a grotto can take hours of cycling back and forth
7. **Save before entering** if you see contents -- the Pokemon's stats/gender are determined when you interact

## Summary: Gen 5 Collector Priorities

### What Gen 5 Uniquely Offers

1. **Hidden Grotto HA specimens** -- The only current Gen 5 source for 17 Gen 1 HA species (see Section C.1)
2. **Dream Ball specimens** -- Historical Dream World catches and Dream Radar catches (Slowpoke, Staryu, Porygon)
3. **B2W2 move tutor moves** -- Last pre-Bank tutor set, includes Snatch (legacy), Stealth Rock, Knock Off, and many competitive staples
4. **Shiny Charm + Masuda Method breeding** -- 1/1024 odds in B2W2 (best pre-Gen 6 shiny rate)
5. **Imposter Ditto** -- Hidden Grotto exclusive in Gen 5
6. **100% nature control via Everstone** -- B2W2 guarantees nature inheritance (vs 50% in BW)

### What Gen 5 Does NOT Offer

1. **Ribbons** -- Zero in-game ribbons; only post-Gen 3 generation with this gap
2. **Ball variety** -- No Apricorn Balls, no Safari Balls, no Sport Balls (those are Gen 4 HGSS)
3. **Most Gen 1 wild encounters** -- Majority of Gen 1 species require transfer from Gen 4
4. **Reliable HA access for most species** -- Dream World closure leaves only Hidden Grottos and Dream Radar

### Recommended Collection Order

1. Play through B2W2 (more Gen 1 access than BW)
2. Farm Hidden Grottos for priority HA specimens (Ditto, Vulpix, Nidoran, Pinsir, Tangela, Clefairy)
3. Use Dream Radar for Slowpoke, Staryu, Porygon in Dream Balls with HA
4. Transfer specimens from Gen 4 (HGSS Apricorn Ball specimens) via Poke Transfer
5. Teach B2W2 move tutor moves to all specimens that need them
6. If shiny hunting: complete National Dex for Shiny Charm, breed with Masuda Method (1/1024)
7. When satisfied, use Poke Transporter to send Box 1 to Pokemon Bank
8. From Bank: withdraw to Gen 6/7, or transfer to Pokemon HOME
