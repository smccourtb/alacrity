# LGPE (Let's Go, Pikachu! & Let's Go, Eevee!) Collector Research

Pokemon Let's Go, Pikachu! and Let's Go, Eevee! (2018, Nintendo Switch) are a **walled ecosystem** -- a reimagining of Pokemon Yellow with Pokemon Go catching mechanics, no breeding, no held items, no abilities in the traditional sense, and heavily restricted transfer options. LGPE is the simplest modern Pokemon game from a collector perspective, but its isolation creates unique value propositions.

**The headline features are the walled garden transfer system and catch combo shiny hunting.** LGPE Pokemon carry a unique origin mark, and once they leave for Sword/Shield or later games via HOME, they can never return. The catch combo system makes LGPE one of the most efficient shiny hunting platforms in the series, with overworld shiny visibility eliminating blind encounters entirely.

**LGPE contains exactly 153 species:** the original 151 Kanto Pokemon, plus Meltan and Melmetal (introduced alongside these games). Alolan forms of applicable Gen 1 species are obtainable via in-game NPC trades. There is no National Dex expansion -- this is strictly a Kanto game.

For Gen 7 mechanics (Alolan form breeding, Beast Balls, VC transfer reception), see [09-gen7-sm-usum.md](09-gen7-sm-usum.md). LGPE shares almost nothing with SM/USUM mechanically despite covering the same region and Alolan forms.

## Section A: Game-Wide Mechanics

### A.1 Catching Mechanics (FUNDAMENTAL CHANGE)

LGPE replaces wild battles entirely with Pokemon Go-style catching. Wild Pokemon appear in the overworld (visible before encounter), and the encounter screen presents a throwing interface rather than a battle.

#### A.1.1 How Catching Works

- Pokemon appear in the overworld with visible size variations and (critically) visible shiny sparkles
- Touching a Pokemon initiates a catch encounter -- no weakening via battle
- The player throws Poke Balls using motion controls (docked/Joy-Con) or button press (handheld)
- A shrinking colored ring indicates catch difficulty: green (easy), yellow (medium), orange (hard), red (very hard)
- Throw quality bonuses: **Nice** (1.5x), **Great** (2x), **Excellent** (2.5x) -- landing inside the ring at smaller sizes gives better bonuses
- Pokemon can flee from the encounter (breaks catch combo -- see A.2)
- Berry feeding (Razz, Nanab, Golden Razz) increases catch rates or calms movement
- Co-op mode: second player can throw simultaneously for a "Synchronized Bonus" improving catch chance

#### A.1.2 What This Means for Collectors

- **No false swipe / status moves** -- catching is purely throw-based
- **Ball selection matters less** -- Ultra Balls are best, but the throw quality multiplier dominates
- **No abilities** -- wild Pokemon don't use abilities or moves during encounters
- **Overworld visibility** -- you can see and select targets before committing to an encounter, including size and shiny status

#### A.1.3 Poke Balls Available

LGPE has the most restricted ball selection in any modern Pokemon game:

| Ball | Acquisition | Catch Rate |
|------|------------|------------|
| Poke Ball | Shops, from start | 1x |
| Great Ball | Shops, after Lavender Town | 1.5x |
| Ultra Ball | Shops, after Fuchsia City | 2x |
| Premier Ball | Free with every 10 Poke Balls purchased | 1x |
| Master Ball | Gift from Silph Co. President (1 only) | Guaranteed |
| Cherish Ball | Event distributions only | N/A |

**Collector note:** The Premier Ball is the only "prestige" catching option. With only 6 ball types total (and Cherish Ball being event-only), ball variety collecting is trivial compared to other games. There are no Apricorn Balls, no Beast Balls, no Shop Balls beyond the basics.

### A.2 Catch Combos (PRIMARY PROGRESSION SYSTEM)

Catch combos are the central mechanic of LGPE -- catching consecutive Pokemon of the **same species** builds a chain that progressively improves shiny odds, IV guarantees, rare spawn rates, and experience.

#### A.2.1 Building a Combo

- Catch the same species repeatedly without interruption
- The combo counter appears in the upper-left during encounters

**What breaks a combo:**
- A wild Pokemon **flees** from you
- You catch a **different species**
- You **exit the game** (save and quit)

**What does NOT break a combo:**
- Running away from an encounter (you can safely flee)
- Changing locations/routes
- Encountering (but not catching) a different species

#### A.2.2 Combo Threshold Bonuses

| Chain Length | EXP Multiplier | Guaranteed Perfect IVs | Chained Species Spawn Boost | Rare Spawn Chance |
|-------------|---------------|----------------------|---------------------------|------------------|
| 0-10 | x1 | 0 | x1 | Base |
| 11-20 | x1.5 | 2 | x3 | 30% |
| 21-30 | x2 | 3 | x5 | 40% |
| 31-40 | x2.5 | 4 | x6 | 50% |
| 41+ | x3 | 4 (capped) | x6 (capped) | 50% (capped) |

**Key thresholds:** 11 (first meaningful bonus), 31 (shiny and IV cap -- this is the magic number).

#### A.2.3 IV Guarantees

At a catch combo of 31+, every Pokemon of the chained species that spawns will have **4 guaranteed perfect IVs** (31 in four random stats). Combined with the Celadon City fortune teller for nature control (see A.6), this makes LGPE one of the easiest games in the series for obtaining competitive-ready specimens -- no breeding required.

### A.3 Shiny Hunting (BEST IN SERIES)

LGPE is arguably the best shiny hunting platform in the entire series due to the combination of catch combos, overworld visibility, and stacking bonuses.

#### A.3.1 Shiny Odds Table

| Combo Level | Base | + Lure | + Shiny Charm | + Lure + Charm |
|------------|------|--------|--------------|---------------|
| 0-10 | 1/4096 | 1/2048 | 1/1365 | 1/1024 |
| 11-20 | 1/1024 | 1/819 | 1/683 | 1/585 |
| 21-30 | 1/512 | 1/455 | 1/410 | 1/372 |
| 31+ | 1/341 | 1/315 | 1/293 | **1/273** |

**Best possible odds: 1/273** (Catch combo 31+ / Lure active / Shiny Charm). This is among the best non-guaranteed shiny rates in the series.

**Important mechanic:** The combo shiny bonus only applies to the **next spawn** of the chained species after a catch. You must keep catching to keep the bonus active -- simply standing with a combo of 31 does not passively apply the bonus to all spawns indefinitely.

#### A.3.2 Overworld Shiny Visibility

Shiny Pokemon are **visible in the overworld** before you touch them -- they display sparkle effects. This means:
- You can chain one species to build your combo, then **watch the overworld** for a shiny of any species to appear
- No blind encounters -- you will never accidentally run from a shiny you didn't see
- Route transitions and entering/exiting buildings refresh spawns, useful for spawn manipulation

#### A.3.3 Shiny Charm

Obtained from the **Game Director** in Celadon City's Celadon Condominiums after completing the Kanto Pokedex (all 150 species caught -- Mew not required). Adds 2 extra shiny rolls to all encounters.

#### A.3.4 Lures

Three tiers of Lure items increase spawn rates and add 1 extra shiny roll:

| Lure Type | Duration | Effect |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Lure | ~100 steps | +1 shiny roll, increased spawns |
| Super Lure | ~200 steps | +1 shiny roll, increased spawns |
| Max Lure | ~250 steps | +1 shiny roll, increased spawns |

All three provide the same shiny bonus -- Max Lures simply last longer.

#### A.3.5 Shiny Locks

The following are **shiny locked** in LGPE:
- **Partner Pikachu** (Let's Go, Pikachu!)
- **Partner Eevee** (Let's Go, Eevee!)
- **Mew** (Poke Ball Plus gift -- cannot be shiny)
- **In-game trade Alolan forms** (NPC trades are never shiny)

The following **can** be shiny:
- All wild encounters (including rare spawns)
- Legendary birds (Articuno, Zapdos, Moltres) -- both static and overworld post-game encounters
- Mewtwo (Cerulean Cave static encounter)
- Gift Pokemon (Bulbasaur, Charmander, Squirtle, Lapras, fossil Pokemon, etc.) -- these can be soft-reset for shiny **[NEEDS VERIFICATION]**
- Go Park transfers (retain shiny status from Pokemon Go)

### A.4 Go Park (POKEMON GO INTEGRATION)

Go Park replaces the Safari Zone in Fuchsia City and serves as the bridge between Pokemon Go and LGPE.

#### A.4.1 Transfer Process

1. Connect Pokemon Go account to Switch via Bluetooth
2. Select Pokemon to send from Go to LGPE (one-way -- cannot return to Go)
3. Transferred Pokemon appear in the Go Park complex
4. Walk up to them in the park and catch them using standard LGPE catching mechanics

#### A.4.2 Eligible Pokemon

- All 151 Kanto Pokemon (except Mew)
- Alolan forms of Kanto species
- Meltan and Melmetal
- **Cannot transfer:** Mew, Shadow Pokemon, Purified Pokemon, costumed/event forms

#### A.4.3 Stats and Data Conversion

| Attribute | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **IVs** | Formula: LGPE IV = (2 x Go IV) + 1 for HP/Atk/Def/SpAtk/SpDef; Speed is random |
| **Nature** | Randomly generated when encountered in Go Park (use Celadon fortune teller to control) |
| **Ball** | Original Go ball is overridden -- recaptured in whatever ball you use in LGPE |
| **Shiny status** | Preserved from Pokemon Go |
| **Level** | Based on CP in Go |
| **Moves** | Reset to level-appropriate moveset |

**IV note:** Because the formula doubles Go IVs (0-15 range) and adds 1, transferred Pokemon always have **odd-numbered IVs** (1, 3, 5, ... 31) for HP through SpDef. Speed is independently random. A perfect Go Pokemon (15/15/15) arrives with 31/31/31/31/31/random.

#### A.4.4 Go Park Capacity

20 individual parks, each holding 50 Pokemon = **1,000 total capacity**. When 25 of the same species are in a single park, a minigame ("Play Yard") becomes available.

#### A.4.5 Mystery Box (Meltan Source)

Transferring any Pokemon from Go to LGPE awards a **Mystery Box** in Pokemon Go. Opening it spawns wild Meltan in Go for 60 minutes. The box has a 3-day cooldown before it can be reopened (by transferring another Pokemon). Meltan evolves into Melmetal with 400 Meltan Candy **in Pokemon Go only** -- there is no way to evolve Meltan in LGPE itself.

### A.5 Transfer Restrictions (CRITICAL -- WALLED GARDEN)

LGPE operates as a **walled garden** within the Pokemon transfer ecosystem. This is the single most important thing collectors need to understand about these games.

#### A.5.1 Transfer Routes

```
Pokemon Go  ------>  LGPE  ------>  Pokemon HOME  ------>  SwSh / SV / etc.
(one-way)           (one-way out)    (one-way to newer games)

Pokemon HOME  <----->  LGPE
(bidirectional, BUT only for LGPE-compatible Pokemon that have NEVER been in SwSh/SV/etc.)
```

#### A.5.2 The Rules

1. **LGPE to HOME:** Any LGPE Pokemon can be sent to HOME (direct Switch-to-Switch transfer, no Bank involved)
2. **HOME to LGPE:** Only Pokemon that are LGPE-compatible can return. This means:
   - Must be one of the 153 LGPE species (Kanto 151 + Meltan + Melmetal) or applicable Alolan forms
   - Must **never** have entered Sword/Shield, Scarlet/Violet, BDSP, or Legends: Arceus
   - Once a Pokemon has been withdrawn into ANY Gen 8/9 game, it is permanently flagged and **cannot return to LGPE**
3. **Go to LGPE:** One-way via Go Park (see A.4)
4. **LGPE has NO connection to Pokemon Bank** -- Bank is 3DS infrastructure only, LGPE is Switch

#### A.5.3 The Permanence Problem

The walled garden means:
- Sending an LGPE Pokemon to SwSh "just to check something" permanently locks it out of LGPE
- There is no undo -- the game data format conversion is one-way
- Carefully manage HOME boxes to keep LGPE-returnable Pokemon separate from Gen 8/9 Pokemon
- **Plan your transfers before executing them**

#### A.5.4 Collector Implication

An LGPE Pokemon that has never left LGPE (or only visited HOME without entering another game) retains maximum flexibility. Once it enters SwSh or later, it gains the capabilities of that game (dynamax, etc.) but permanently loses LGPE residency.

### A.6 Nature Control (FORTUNE TELLER)

Since LGPE has no breeding and no Synchronize ability, nature control is handled by a unique NPC.

**Madam Celadon** -- a Channeler located in the Celadon City Pokemon Center -- will set the nature of all subsequently encountered wild Pokemon for **10,000 Poke Dollars**. She asks two questions:

1. Which stat should be **increased** (determines the + stat)
2. Which stat should be **decreased** (determines the - stat)

The effect lasts for the **rest of the in-game day** and applies to:
- All wild encounters
- Gift Pokemon
- Go Park encounters
- Fossil revivals

**Does NOT apply to:** Mew (Poke Ball Plus).

This is the **only** nature control method in LGPE. Combined with catch combo IV guarantees, you can efficiently farm competitive specimens: set the desired nature via fortune teller, build a catch combo to 31+, and every catch will have 4 perfect IVs and the right nature.

### A.7 Awakening Values (AVs -- REPLACES EVs)

LGPE replaces Effort Values with Awakening Values, a candy-based stat boost system.

#### A.7.1 Core Mechanics

- Each stat (HP, Atk, Def, SpAtk, SpDef, Spe) can have up to **200 AVs**
- Each AV provides **+1 to the stat** regardless of level (unlike EVs where 4 EVs = 1 stat point at level 100)
- There is **no total cap** -- all six stats can be maxed at 200 simultaneously (1,200 total AVs)
- AVs are applied via **candy** items obtained from catching Pokemon and transferring to Professor Oak

#### A.7.2 Candy Tiers

| Candy Type | Level Requirement | Effective AV Range | Notes |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|-------|
| Regular (e.g., Health Candy) | Level 1+ | 0-50 AVs | Escalating cost per point |
| L Candy (e.g., Health Candy L) | Level 30+ | 50-100 AVs | Escalating cost per point |
| XL Candy (e.g., Health Candy XL) | Level 60+ | 100-200 AVs | Escalating cost per point |
| Species-Specific (e.g., Pikachu Candy) | Level 1+ | 0-200 AVs | +1 to ALL stats per candy |

**Species-specific candy** is the most efficient: one candy adds +1 AV to every stat simultaneously, with no escalating cost. Obtained primarily through catch combos (catching many of one species yields its candy) and transferring Pokemon to Professor Oak.

#### A.7.3 Collector Significance

- AVs are **LGPE-exclusive** -- they do not transfer to other games
- A fully AV-trained specimen has up to +200 in every stat, making it absurdly powerful within LGPE
- When transferred to HOME/SwSh, AVs are discarded -- the Pokemon arrives with 0 EVs
- **No competitive preservation reason to keep AV-trained Pokemon in LGPE** unless you value in-game power

### A.8 No Breeding

LGPE has **no Daycare breeding, no eggs, no egg moves, no egg groups**. This is a fundamental departure from every mainline game since Gen 2.

**Collector implications:**
- No ball inheritance -- you must catch every specimen in the desired ball directly
- No IV breeding -- use catch combos for IV optimization instead
- No egg move specimens -- irrelevant since LGPE's move pool is simplified
- No HA breeding -- abilities don't exist in LGPE (see A.9)

---

### A.9 No Abilities / No Held Items

LGPE removes both traditional abilities and held items entirely.

- Pokemon have **no ability** in LGPE. When transferred to HOME and then to a game with abilities, they receive their **standard ability** (not Hidden Ability)
- Items exist in the bag but **cannot be given to Pokemon to hold**
- Mega Stones work differently: they activate from the **bag** during battle rather than being held (see A.10)

### A.11 Origin Mark

LGPE Pokemon receive the **Let's Go mark** -- a silhouette resembling both Pikachu and Eevee heads with large ears. This mark is visible in Pokemon HOME and later games but does **not** display within LGPE itself.

This is a **unique origin mark** shared by no other game. It visually distinguishes LGPE-origin specimens from those caught in any other generation.

Both are transferable to HOME and onward to SwSh (Melmetal can even Gigantamax in SwSh with the right source).

## Section C: Collector Value Assessment

### C.1 Why Keep Pokemon in LGPE?

The central question: is there any reason to maintain specimens in LGPE rather than transferring them out?

**Reasons to keep in LGPE:**
- **Master Trainer challenges** (see C.3) -- require species-matched 1v1 battles
- **AV-trained specimens** -- AVs don't transfer and maxing them creates powerful in-game Pokemon
- **Mega Evolution availability** -- LGPE is one of the last games with Mega Evolution
- **Ongoing shiny hunts** -- LGPE remains one of the best shiny hunting platforms

**Reasons to transfer out:**
- The **Let's Go origin mark** is only visible in HOME and later games, not in LGPE itself
- LGPE specimens gain access to abilities, held items, breeding, and broader competitive play in SwSh/SV
- HOME provides cloud backup that LGPE save data lacks
- Most collectors want specimens consolidated in one location

### C.2 The Let's Go Origin Mark as Collector Value

The Let's Go mark is unique and cannot be obtained in any other game. For collectors who value origin diversity, an LGPE-origin specimen is a distinct collectible. However:
- The mark is cosmetic -- it confers no gameplay benefit
- It proves the Pokemon was caught or received in LGPE specifically
- Combined with Premier Ball + nature + shiny, an LGPE-origin specimen can be a prestige item

### C.3 Master Trainers (Post-Game)

153 Master Trainers appear throughout Kanto after becoming Champion -- **one for each species** (151 Kanto + Meltan + Melmetal). They wear red and have their species icon displayed above their heads.

**Battle rules:**
- You must use the **same species** as the Master Trainer
- 1v1 format, **no items** allowed during battle
- Their Pokemon are high-level with strong AVs and good IVs
- Defeating a Master Trainer earns a title (e.g., "Pikachu Master")

**Collector relevance:** Master Trainers are the primary post-game content in LGPE and the main reason to keep strong specimens in-game rather than transferring immediately. Completing all 153 Master Trainer challenges is a significant accomplishment, and you need one well-trained specimen per species to do it.

### C.4 What LGPE Cannot Do

For collector context, LGPE is missing many standard features:
- No breeding / no eggs
- No held items
- No abilities
- No GTS or Wonder Trade (local/online link trades only)
- No move tutors beyond partner exclusive moves
- No Hidden Abilities (abilities don't exist in LGPE; transferred Pokemon receive standard ability)
- No EVs (AVs instead, which don't transfer)
- No Pokerus
- No Battle Frontier / Battle facility beyond Master Trainers
- No connection to Pokemon Bank

## Section D: Walkthrough and Transfer Strategy

### D.1 Recommended Progression for Collectors

1. **Start game** -- receive Partner Pikachu/Eevee (note: these are permanent special Pokemon, not transferable specimens)
2. **Play through story** -- catch everything you encounter, building toward gift Pokemon thresholds (30/50/60 caught)
3. **Collect all gift Pokemon** as they become available (Bulbasaur, Charmander, Squirtle, Lapras, Hitmonlee/Hitmonchan, Porygon, fossils)
4. **Complete Alolan form trades** at each Pokemon Center as you progress through Kanto
5. **Defeat Elite Four** to unlock post-game
6. **Catch Mewtwo** in Cerulean Cave
7. **Obtain all Mega Stones** from Blue (Pallet Town) and the Stone Salesman (Indigo Plateau)
8. **Complete the Pokedex** (150 species) to obtain the **Shiny Charm**
9. **Begin shiny hunting** with full combo + Charm + Lure setup

### D.2 Shiny Hunting Strategy

The optimal LGPE shiny hunting loop:

1. Set nature via Madam Celadon in Celadon City (10,000 per day)
2. Activate a Max Lure
3. Build a catch combo to **31** with the target species (or any common species if hunting a rare spawn)
4. Once at 31+, watch the overworld for shiny sparkles on your target species
5. Continue catching to maintain the shiny bonus on subsequent spawns
6. When a shiny appears in the overworld, engage it carefully -- **do not let it flee** (use Razz Berries, Ultra Balls)

**Tip:** For stationary shiny hunting (legendaries, Snorlax), save before the encounter and soft-reset. The combo bonus does not affect static encounters, but the Shiny Charm and Lure do apply.

### D.3 Before Transferring Out -- Checklist

Complete these activities **before** sending specimens to HOME, as they cannot return once they enter a Gen 8/9 game:

- [ ] Complete all Master Trainer challenges you care about
- [ ] Max AVs on any specimens you want to use in LGPE long-term

### D.4 Transfer Restrictions Reminder

Before transferring any LGPE Pokemon:

1. **LGPE to HOME** -- always safe, bidirectional as long as the Pokemon never enters another game
2. **HOME to SwSh/SV/etc.** -- **PERMANENT**. The Pokemon can never return to LGPE.
3. **Go Park imports** -- one-way from Go, nature is random (set fortune teller first)
4. **No Bank connection** -- LGPE exists entirely in the Switch/HOME ecosystem

**The safest approach:** Keep LGPE specimens in HOME's LGPE-dedicated boxes until you are certain you want them in a newer game. Once they enter SwSh or later, the door back to LGPE closes forever.
