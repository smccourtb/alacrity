# Gen 6 (XY + ORAS) Collector Research

Generation VI is a **transformative generation for collectors** -- Pokemon X/Y (2013) and Omega Ruby/Alpha Sapphire (2014) introduced **Mega Evolution**, **Pokemon Bank** (cloud-based transfer backbone), **Destiny Knot 5-IV breeding**, **Friend Safari** (XY's primary HA source), **DexNav** (ORAS's chaining system), and a halved base shiny rate (1/4096). ORAS also brought back **Contest Spectacular** and a full roster of **move tutors**, making it the definitive Gen 6 game for competitive and collector preparation.

**The headline features are Mega Evolution and Pokemon Bank.** Thirteen Gen 1 species received Mega forms across XY and ORAS, creating a new axis of collector interest. Pokemon Bank established the cloud-based transfer pipeline that persists through HOME today -- Gen 5 -> Bank -> Gen 6/7 -> HOME is how pre-3DS Pokemon reach modern games.

Gen 6 also **halved the base shiny rate** from 1/8192 to 1/4096, introduced Chain Fishing (the fastest shiny method in the series), and made Destiny Knot breeding pass 5 IVs instead of 3 -- a game-changer for breeding perfect competitive specimens.

For Gen 5 mechanics (Hidden Abilities, Dream World, Hidden Grottos, Shiny Charm), see [07-gen5-bw-b2w2.md](07-gen5-bw-b2w2.md). This document assumes familiarity with those systems and focuses on what Gen 6 adds or changes.

## Section A: Game-Wide Mechanics

### A.1 Mega Evolution (NEW -- MAJOR)

Mega Evolution is a temporary, in-battle transformation introduced in Gen 6. A Pokemon holding its species-specific Mega Stone can Mega Evolve once per battle if the Trainer has a Key Stone. Mega Evolution changes stats, type (sometimes), and ability. The transformation lasts only for that battle.

**Collector significance:** Mega Stones are held items, not permanent modifications to the Pokemon itself. However, a specimen intended to Mega Evolve should ideally have the right nature/IVs for its Mega form's stat spread and ability, which may differ from its base form's optimal build.

### A.2 Pokemon Bank (NEW -- CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE)

Pokemon Bank is a 3DS cloud storage application that became the **backbone of the modern transfer system**. It launched December 25, 2013 (Japan) and February 4-5, 2014 (worldwide).

#### A.2.1 What Bank Does

- **Cloud storage** for up to 3,000 Pokemon across 100 boxes
- **Connects to** Gen 6 (XY, ORAS) and Gen 7 (SM, USUM) games
- **Poke Transporter** is a companion app that transfers Pokemon one-way from:
  - Gen 5 games (BW, B2W2) -> Bank
  - Virtual Console Gen 1 (RBY) -> Bank
  - Virtual Console Gen 2 (GSC) -> Bank
- **Bank -> HOME** transfer (one-way, added later in Bank v1.5)

#### A.2.2 The Transfer Chain

This is the critical pipeline for moving pre-3DS Pokemon to modern games:

```
Gen 3 (RSE/FRLG) -> Gen 4 (DPPt/HGSS) via Pal Park
Gen 4 -> Gen 5 (BW/B2W2) via Poke Transfer
Gen 5 -> Bank via Poke Transporter
VC Gen 1/2 -> Bank via Poke Transporter
Bank -> Gen 6/7 games (bidirectional within Bank)
Bank -> HOME (one-way)
HOME -> Gen 8/9/Legends games (one-way per game)
```

#### A.2.3 Current Status

- Bank became **free to use on March 27, 2023** when the 3DS eShop shut down
- Bank and Poke Transporter are the **only 3DS software** to retain online functionality after Nintendo ended 3DS online services (April 8, 2024)
- Users who downloaded Bank before the eShop closure can continue using it indefinitely
- **Cannot be newly downloaded** -- new 3DS owners cannot access Bank

**Collector warning:** Bank is the single point of failure for the Gen 5 -> Gen 6+ and VC -> modern transfer routes. If Nintendo ever shuts it down, those transfer paths are permanently severed.

### A.3 Origin Marks (NEW)

Gen 6 introduced the **origin mark system** -- visual indicators of where a Pokemon was originally obtained.

| Origin Mark | Shape | Games | Notes |
|-------------|-------|-------|-------|
| **Blue Pentagon** | Pentagon (Kalos shape) | XY, ORAS | First origin mark ever; blue in Gen 6/Bank, black from Gen 7+ |
| **Game Boy** | Stylized Game Boy | VC RBY/GSC via Bank | Added when VC Pokemon are transferred through Bank |
| No mark | -- | Gen 3-5 transfers | Pokemon from RSE/FRLG/DPPt/HGSS/BW/B2W2 receive **no origin mark** |

**Key details:**
- XY and ORAS Pokemon both get the same Blue Pentagon -- you cannot distinguish Kalos-born from Hoenn-born by mark alone
- The internal "game of origin" data does track which specific game a Pokemon came from
- All 721 Pokemon obtainable across Gen 6 can bear the Pentagon
- Pokemon transferred INTO Gen 6 from Gen 5 via Bank do **not** receive the Pentagon -- they have no origin mark

### A.4 Destiny Knot Breeding (GAME-CHANGER)

The Destiny Knot received a massive upgrade in Gen 6 that revolutionized competitive breeding.

#### A.4.1 Mechanics

| Mechanic | Gen 5 | Gen 6 |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **IV inheritance** (no item) | 3 random IVs from parents | 3 random IVs from parents |
| **IV inheritance** (Destiny Knot) | N/A (Destiny Knot had no breeding effect) | **5 random IVs from parents** |
| **Nature passing** (Everstone) | 100% | 100% |
| **HA passing** (female + male) | 60% (BW) / 80% (B2W2) | 60% |
| **HA passing** (any gender + Ditto) | **Not possible** | **60%** (NEW) |
| **Ball inheritance** | Always Poke Ball | **Mother's ball** (NEW) |
| **Egg moves from mother** | Father only | **Both parents** (NEW) |

#### A.4.2 Optimal Breeding Setup

The ideal Gen 6 breeding pair:
1. **Parent A:** Target species, female, in desired ball, with HA (if wanted), holding **Everstone** (passes nature)
2. **Parent B:** Compatible species or Ditto with 6 perfect IVs, holding **Destiny Knot** (passes 5 IVs)

With two 6-IV parents + Destiny Knot, every egg inherits 5 of the 6 perfect IVs -- only 1 IV is randomly generated. The odds of a perfect 6-IV offspring are roughly **1 in 32** (1/31 chance the random IV is also 31).

#### A.4.3 HA Passing Changes (Critical)

**The biggest breeding change for collectors:** In Gen 6, **males and genderless Pokemon can pass their Hidden Ability when bred with Ditto.** In Gen 5, only females could pass HA, making HA breeding impossible for genderless species and extremely difficult for male-skewed species (87.5% male starters).

This means:
- HA Kanto starters (Chlorophyll Bulbasaur, Solar Power Charmander, Rain Dish Squirtle) can now be bred from a single male HA specimen + Ditto
- Genderless HA Pokemon (like Magnemite with Analytic) can now breed their HA
- The HA passing rate is **60%** regardless of parent gender when breeding with Ditto

#### A.4.4 Ball Inheritance

Gen 6 introduced Poke Ball inheritance in breeding:
- **Female parent's ball** is passed to offspring (when breeding male + female)
- When breeding **any Pokemon + Ditto**, the non-Ditto parent's ball is passed
- **Master Ball and Cherish Ball** cannot be inherited (offspring gets standard Poke Ball instead)
- This is the first generation where ball inheritance exists -- specimens caught in desirable balls (Luxury Ball, matching-color balls, etc.) gain collector value

**Gen 6 limitation:** Only the mother's ball passes in male + female breeding. Gen 7 later allows either parent's ball to pass (50/50).

### A.5 Shiny Hunting Methods and Odds

Gen 6 **halved the base shiny rate** and introduced several new hunting methods.

| Method | Odds (no Charm) | Odds (w/ Charm) | Games | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|------------------|-------|-------|
| **Random encounter** | 1/4,096 | 1/1,365 | XY, ORAS | Base rate halved from Gen 5's 1/8,192 |
| **Masuda Method** | 1/683 | **1/512** | XY, ORAS | Optimal breeding method |
| **Chain Fishing** | ~1/100 at 20+ chain | ~1/96 | XY, ORAS | Fastest shiny method in the series |
| **Poke Radar** | ~1/200 at 40+ chain | Better w/ Charm | XY only | Returns from Gen 4; grass-only |
| **DexNav chaining** | Improves with chain | Best at 50/100+ | ORAS only | Combined with Search Level |
| **Friend Safari** | 1/819 (estimated) | 1/585 (estimated) | XY only | Fixed elevated rate in all Friend Safaris |
| **Horde encounters** | 5x 1/4,096 per horde | 5x 1/1,365 | XY, ORAS | 5 Pokemon = 5 rolls per encounter |

#### A.5.1 Chain Fishing (XY/ORAS)

Chain Fishing is remarkably simple:
1. Stand in one spot near water
2. Cast your rod repeatedly without moving
3. Each consecutive hook adds +2 rerolls to the shiny check
4. At 20+ consecutive catches, you have **41 rerolls** (capped)
5. This produces roughly a **1 in 100 (~1%)** shiny chance per encounter

**Chain breaks if:** You fail to get a nibble, move from your spot, or encounter a shiny (resets chain). Defeat/capture/flee do NOT break the chain.

**Gen 1 species targetable by Chain Fishing:** Any Gen 1 water-encounter species -- Magikarp, Goldeen, Poliwag, Tentacool, Horsea, Staryu, Gyarados, Seaking, etc.

#### A.5.2 Shiny Charm

The Shiny Charm is obtained by **completing the National Pokedex** (all 718 Pokemon seen/caught in Gen 6). It adds 2 extra rerolls to every shiny check, stacking with all methods.

### A.6 Friend Safari (XY Only)

The Friend Safari is a post-game area in Kiloude City where registered 3DS friends each correspond to a themed Safari zone. Each friend's Safari has a specific type and contains 2-3 Pokemon species.

#### A.6.1 Mechanics

- All Friend Safari Pokemon are **Level 30**
- All have **2 guaranteed perfect IVs** (3rd-slot Pokemon may have 3)
- All can have their **Hidden Ability** (if the friend has been online in-game with you at least once, unlocking the 3rd slot)
- The **shiny rate is elevated** to approximately 1/819 (or 1/585 with Shiny Charm)
- Pokemon appear in standard Poke Balls you throw -- they are catchable like normal wild encounters

### A.7 DexNav (ORAS Only)

The DexNav is ORAS's signature encounter system. After encountering a species, the DexNav can "search" for it, showing a rustling patch with information about the Pokemon before you engage it.

#### A.7.1 Mechanics

- **Search Level** increases each time you encounter a species (caps at 999)
- **Chain count** increases with consecutive encounters of the same species via DexNav
- Higher Search Levels and chains unlock bonuses:

| Bonus | Search Level 0-9 | Search Level 25+ | Search Level 100+ |
|-------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Hidden Ability chance | 0% | ~10% | ~25% |
| Egg Move chance | ~20% | ~50% | ~80% |
| Perfect IV chance | 0% | ~5% | ~10% |
| Held Item (rare) | ~10% | ~20% | ~30% |

- Chains break if you encounter a different species, flee, or the Pokemon flees
- Each 5 consecutive encounters raise the encountered level by 1 (up to +20)

#### A.7.2 DexNav Shiny Hunting

DexNav chaining does increase shiny odds, with the best rates at chain milestones of 50 and 100+. Combined with a high Search Level and the Shiny Charm, DexNav chaining can achieve rates as favorable as approximately **1/36** at optimal conditions (Search Level 100+, chain 100, Shiny Charm).

**Collector note:** DexNav is the primary method for HA hunting in ORAS, since ORAS has no Friend Safari.

### A.9 Poke Balls

Gen 6 has the **full modern ball selection**:
- All standard shop balls (Poke, Great, Ultra, Premier, etc.)
- Specialty balls (Timer, Quick, Dusk, Luxury, Heal, Net, Dive, Nest, Repeat)
- **Ball inheritance** exists for the first time (mother's ball passes to offspring)
- **Master Ball** (1 per game, does not pass through breeding)

**Apricorn Balls are NOT available in Gen 6.** Apricorn Ball Pokemon can only enter Gen 6 via Bank transfer from HGSS.

**Ball collecting note:** Gen 6 is the first generation where catching a Pokemon in a specific ball has long-term significance through breeding. A Lapras in a Dive Ball, for example, can breed that ball onto all future Lapras offspring.

### A.11 Contest Spectacular (ORAS Only)

ORAS features **Contest Spectacular**, an updated version of Gen 3's Pokemon Contests. Contests take place in Contest Halls and have 5 categories: **Coolness, Beauty, Cuteness, Cleverness, Toughness**.

- Each category has 4 ranks: Normal, Super, Hyper, Master
- Ribbons are only awarded for **Master Rank** victories
- Pokemon can skip lower ranks once any Pokemon in your party has cleared them
- Contests use a performance stage with audience voting (no Contest Battle round)
- **Pokeblocks** can be made from Berries to raise contest condition stats
- **Cosplay Pikachu** (ORAS gift) has special contest appeal

**Collector action:** If any specimen will be a ribbon master candidate, complete all 5 Master Rank contests in ORAS before transferring forward.

**Roaming bird mechanics:** After entering the Hall of Fame, one legendary bird begins roaming Kalos. Which bird depends on your **Kalos starter** (not Kanto starter):
- Chespin -> **Articuno** (Ice counters Grass)
- Fennekin -> **Zapdos** (Electric counters Fire's Water weakness)
- Froakie -> **Moltres** (Fire counters Water's Grass weakness)

The bird always **flees immediately** before you can act. After encountering it approximately 10-12 times across Kalos, it settles at **Sea Spirit's Den** where you can battle and catch it at Level 70.

**Mewtwo** sits in the Unknown Dungeon (accessed from Pokemon Village), a direct callback to Cerulean Cave. It holds either Mewtwonite X (in X) or Mewtwonite Y (in Y).

**Only one legendary bird per save file.** To get all three birds natively in Gen 6, you need three separate XY saves with different starter choices, or trade.

## Section D: Mega Evolution Collector Implications

### D.1 Practical Considerations

For each Gen 1 Mega Evolution species, collectors should consider:

1. **Nature optimization:** The optimal nature may differ between base form and Mega form (e.g., base Gyarados wants Adamant for physical Water/Flying; Mega Gyarados still wants Adamant but for Water/Dark with Mold Breaker)
2. **IV spread:** Most Megas want 5-6 perfect IVs. With Destiny Knot breeding, this is achievable
3. **Mega Stone is a held item:** The Pokemon itself is not permanently changed. One specimen can serve as both a base and Mega user
4. **Ball choice:** Since ball inheritance now exists, breed specimens in aesthetically matching balls

### D.2 Specimens Worth Special Attention

| Mega | Why Notable | Recommended Investment |
|------|-------------|----------------------|
| Mega Charizard X | Type changes to Fire/Dragon; physical attacker with Tough Claws | Breed Jolly/Adamant with good Attack/Speed IVs |
| Mega Charizard Y | Special sweeper with auto-Drought | Breed Timid/Modest with good SpAtk/Speed IVs |
| Mega Kangaskhan | Parental Bond was the defining mechanic of Gen 6 VGC | Breed Jolly/Adamant |
| Mega Gengar | Shadow Tag trapping; dominant in singles | Breed Timid with SpAtk/Speed |
| Mega Beedrill | Incredible glass cannon (150 Atk, 145 Spe); the biggest glow-up of any Gen 1 species | Breed Jolly with Atk/Speed |
| Mega Mewtwo Y | Highest Special Attack of any Pokemon (194 base SpAtk) | Catch in XY with Timid nature |
| Mega Mewtwo X | Highest Attack of any Psychic-type (190 base Atk) | Catch in XY with Jolly/Adamant nature |

### D.3 Dual Mega Forms (Charizard and Mewtwo)

Collectors aiming for completeness may want to prepare **two specimens** for Charizard and Mewtwo -- one optimized for X form, one for Y form. Since each Pokemon can only Mega Evolve into one form per battle (determined by which Mega Stone it holds), having two differently-built specimens maximizes utility.

## Section E: Breeding

### E.1 Destiny Knot + Everstone Strategy

The standard Gen 6 breeding loop:

1. **Obtain a 6-IV Ditto** (Friend Safari Ditto have 2 guaranteed IVs; Ditto chains/RNG manipulation can yield 6-IV specimens)
2. **Attach Destiny Knot to Ditto**, Everstone to desired-nature parent
3. Breed until 5-IV offspring appear
4. Replace parent with better offspring, repeat until 5-6 IV
5. For HA: ensure the non-Ditto parent has HA (60% pass rate)
6. For ball: ensure the female/non-Ditto parent is in the desired ball

### E.2 Ball Inheritance Specifics

In Gen 6:
- Female + Male breeding: **female's ball always passes**
- Any gender + Ditto: **non-Ditto parent's ball passes**
- Master Ball and Cherish Ball: **cannot pass** (offspring gets Poke Ball)
- **Same-species breeding** in Gen 6 does NOT allow male's ball to pass (this changes in Gen 7)

### E.3 Egg Move Changes

New in Gen 6:
- **Mothers can pass egg moves** (previously father-only)
- Move Relearner can reteach any move a Pokemon knew when it hatched (moves are permanently recorded)
- Several Gen 1 species received new egg moves (see Section F)

### E.4 Masuda Method Optimal Setup

For shiny breeding:
1. Obtain a **foreign-language 6-IV Ditto** (different language tag from your game)
2. Use Ditto + target species parent (with HA, egg moves, correct ball)
3. Attach Destiny Knot to Ditto, Everstone to parent
4. Base Masuda odds: **1/683** (without Charm) / **1/512** (with Charm)
5. This produces shinies that also have good IVs, correct nature, and HA -- the gold standard

### G.3 Cross-Game Coordination

The optimal Gen 6 collector workflow:

1. **Play XY first** for Friend Safari access (HA hunting for Gen 1 species)
2. **Play ORAS second** for move tutors and Contest Spectacular ribbons
3. Transfer Friend Safari HA specimens to ORAS via Bank
4. Teach ORAS tutor moves to all keeper specimens
5. Complete Contest Spectacular ribbons on any ribbon master candidates
6. Teach any remaining XY-only ribbon requirements (Best Friends, Training, etc.) -- note these can also be done in ORAS
7. Transfer to Bank when ready for Gen 7

### G.4 Transfer Checklist -- Before Leaving Gen 6

Before transferring any keeper specimen to Bank (and eventually HOME/Gen 7+), verify:

- [ ] **ORAS Move Tutors:** All desired tutor moves taught (Knock Off, Stealth Rock, elemental punches, etc.)
- [ ] **Contest Ribbons:** All 5 Master Rank + Contest Star Ribbon (if ribbon master)
- [ ] **Champion Ribbons:** Both Kalos Champion (XY) and Hoenn Champion (ORAS) if applicable
- [ ] **Battle Maison Ribbons:** Skillful Battler and Expert Battler
- [ ] **Best Friends Ribbon:** Max Pokemon-Amie affection
- [ ] **Training Ribbon:** All Super Training gold medals
- [ ] **Daily Ribbons:** All 7 day-of-week ribbons collected
- [ ] **Effort Ribbon:** EVs maxed (can be reset with Reset Bag)
- [ ] **Correct Ball:** Bred into desired Poke Ball (Gen 6 is first gen with ball inheritance)
- [ ] **Hidden Ability:** Confirmed via summary screen if HA was desired
- [ ] **IVs:** Checked via Kiloude City IV judge (Gen 6) or Battle Resort judge (ORAS)
- [ ] **Egg Moves:** All desired egg moves in moveset or recorded (relearnable via Move Reminder)

### G.5 What Cannot Be Undone After Transfer

Once a Pokemon leaves Gen 6 via Bank:
- **Cannot return to Gen 6 games** (Bank -> HOME is one-way; HOME -> Gen 6 is not possible)
- **Cannot learn ORAS tutor moves** (no tutor equivalent in Gen 7 for some moves)
- **Cannot earn Gen 6 ribbons** (Contest Spectacular, Kalos/Hoenn Champion, etc.)
- **Cannot change ball** (ball inheritance exists in Gen 7+ but requires breeding a new specimen)
