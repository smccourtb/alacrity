# BDSP (Brilliant Diamond & Shining Pearl) Collector Research

Pokemon Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl (2021, Nintendo Switch) are Gen 4 remakes with a **limited collector niche**. They faithfully recreate the Sinnoh region with quality-of-life improvements but lack the standout collector features of HGSS (Apricorn Balls), SwSh (Marks, Gmax), or LGPE (overworld shinies). The headline features are the **Grand Underground** (expanded Underground with Pokemon hideaways providing post-National Dex access to most Gen 1 species), **Ramanas Park** (post-game legendary encounters via Slates), and the return of the **Poke Radar** for shiny chaining.

**All 151 Gen 1 species are obtainable in BDSP** (the game contains 493 Pokemon from Gens 1-4). However, BDSP offers little that cannot be obtained more conveniently elsewhere. The main reasons to source Gen 1 Pokemon from BDSP are: the BDSP origin mark, a handful of ribbons, and Poke Radar shiny hunting.

For original DPPt/HGSS mechanics (physical/special split, Apricorn Balls, Masuda Method introduction), see [06-gen4-dppt-hgss.md](06-gen4-dppt-hgss.md). BDSP shares the same regional dex but is a separate game with its own origin mark and some mechanical differences.

## Section A: Game-Wide Mechanics

### A.1 Origin Mark

BDSP Pokemon receive a unique origin mark: a **triangle with circles at each vertex and center**, representing Sinnoh's three lakes and Mount Coronet. This is distinct from the original DPPt games (which have no origin mark, being pre-HOME era). The BDSP mark is the only way to prove a Pokemon originated specifically in the 2021 remakes.

### A.2 Transfer: BDSP <-> HOME

BDSP gained HOME connectivity on May 18, 2022. Transfer is **bidirectional** for compatible species (Gens 1-4 only, 493 species).

**Key restrictions:**
- Only Gens 1-4 Pokemon can enter BDSP from HOME
- Spinda cannot be transferred to or from BDSP at all
- Pokemon with moves/abilities not present in BDSP have those features flagged on transfer
- Certain legendary Pokemon can only be moved out once per save file
- Pokemon from Legends: Arceus display a "Strange Ball" in BDSP due to ball ID incompatibility

**Collector implication:** BDSP is a dead-end for most purposes. There is no compelling reason to send Pokemon INTO BDSP, and anything caught in BDSP can reach HOME and then any current game. The main value is the origin mark.

### A.3 Ball Availability

### A.4 Hidden Abilities and Ability Patch

- **Ability Patch:** Available from the Battle Park Exchange Service Corner for 200 BP (post-game). Functions identically to SwSh -- changes a Pokemon's ability to its Hidden Ability.
- **Ability Capsule:** Also available from Battle Park.
- **Grand Underground:** Pokemon encountered in hideaways have a 1/128 chance of having their Hidden Ability (indicated by vigorous grass shaking in Poke Radar, and by special spawn behavior in hideaways).
- **Breeding:** Standard modern breeding rules. HA passes from parent with ~60% chance when breeding with Ditto or same-species partner.

### A.5 Breeding Mechanics

Standard Gen 8-era breeding. Daycare is on Route 209 (Solaceon Town).

- Masuda Method: 1/682.7 without Shiny Charm, 1/512 with Shiny Charm
- Destiny Knot passes 5 of 12 combined parent IVs
- Everstone passes nature 100%
- Power Items guarantee specific IV inheritance
- HA inheritance: ~60% from HA parent
- Ball inheritance: female parent's ball (or either parent when breeding with Ditto)

Nothing here is unique to BDSP -- identical mechanics exist in SwSh and SV.

### A.6 Shiny Hunting Methods

#### A.6.1 Wild Encounters
- Base odds: 1/4096 (standard Gen 6+ rate)
- **Shiny Charm does NOT affect wild encounter odds in BDSP** (confirmed bug/design choice -- unlike every other modern game)
- Diglett Bonus (Grand Underground): Collecting 40 Diglett/Dugtrio sparks grants a 4-minute window at 1/2048

#### A.6.2 Poke Radar Chaining
The Poke Radar returns in BDSP and is the primary shiny hunting method. Obtained after completing the National Dex.

**How it works:**
1. Use the Poke Radar in grass (use a Repel first)
2. Four grass patches shake -- walk to one
3. Catch or defeat the Pokemon to continue the chain
4. The Radar reactivates, showing new patches. Continue with the same species
5. Always target the furthest patch (93% continuation rate when catching, vs 63% for nearest)

**Shiny odds by chain length:**

| Chain | Approx. Odds per Patch | Per Radar Use (4 patches) |
|-------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| 1-19 | ~1/4096 to 1/1820 | Low |
| 20 | ~1/1820 | Moderate |
| 30 | ~1/1310 | Good |
| 36 | ~1/1024 | Very good |
| 40+ | **1/99 per patch** | **~1/25 per radar use** |

At chain 40+, you also get 3 guaranteed perfect IVs. Chains can randomly break even with perfect play.

**Shiny Charm does NOT boost Poke Radar odds** despite the item description suggesting otherwise.

#### A.6.3 Masuda Method
Standard breeding odds (see A.5). This is the go-to method for starters and Pokemon not available in grass.

#### A.6.4 Shiny Locks
Only three things are shiny locked:
- Gift Mew (requires LGPE save data)
- Gift Jirachi (requires SwSh save data)
- Mystery Gift distributions

**All legendaries (including Ramanas Park) and starters are NOT shiny locked.** This makes BDSP one of the better games for shiny legendary hunting, though the encounters are standard full-odds (1/4096) with no boosted method like SwSh Dynamax Adventures.

## Section B: Gen 1 Species Availability

### B.1 Grand Underground Hideaways (Primary Source)

The Grand Underground is the primary way to catch Gen 1 species in BDSP. After obtaining the National Pokedex, Pokemon hideaways across 17 biome types become populated with species from all four generations. Pokemon appear as visible overworld sprites in themed rooms.

**Key mechanics:**
- Pokemon levels scale 16-63 based on story progression
- Statue system: placing type-themed statues in your Secret Base boosts spawn rates for that type in hideaways
- Only the type with the most statue points gets boosted (check the orb color at base entrance)
- Rare spawns: each hideaway visit has a small chance of spawning uncommon species
- Egg Moves: underground Pokemon have a chance of knowing an Egg Move at catch

### B.3 Trophy Garden (Daily Rotation)

The Trophy Garden (Route 212, Pokemon Mansion) features daily rotating Pokemon. Speak to Mr. Backlot to advance the rotation, which follows National Dex order.

**Gen 1 species in rotation:**
- Clefairy
- Jigglypuff
- Meowth
- Chansey
- Eevee
- Porygon

The current day's Pokemon and previous day's Pokemon both appear simultaneously. This is the easiest way to catch Porygon and Eevee without Underground hunting.

### B.4 Swarm Pokemon (Daily)

After obtaining the National Dex, Dawn/Lucas's sister in Sandgem Town reports daily swarm locations.

**Gen 1 species in swarms:**
| Pokemon | Swarm Location |
|---------|---------------|
| Pidgey | Route 229 |
| Cubone | Route 203 |

Only two Gen 1 swarm species. These are also available in the Underground, making swarms non-essential.

### B.5 Great Marsh (Pastoria City)

The Great Marsh functions as BDSP's Safari Zone: 500 to enter, 30 Safari Balls, 500-step limit. A binocular mechanic (100 per look) reveals daily rotating species.

**Gen 1 species:**
- Psyduck, Magikarp (regular encounters)
- Exeggcute, Kangaskhan, Paras (post-National Dex binocular rotation)

**Collector note:** Kangaskhan from the Great Marsh comes in a Safari Ball... except Safari Balls are not available as a catch option in BDSP's Great Marsh. Pokemon are caught with regular Safari Balls provided at entry (which display as Safari Balls in Gen 4 data). This is one of the few ways to get Safari Ball Gen 1 Pokemon in BDSP.

### B.6 Ramanas Park (Post-Game Legendaries)

Ramanas Park opens on Route 221 after completing the Sinnoh Dex. Legendary Pokemon are summoned by inserting Slates (purchased with Mysterious Shards from Underground digging) into room pedestals.

**Gen 1 legendaries:**

| Pokemon | Room | Slate | Version | Level | HA |
|---------|------|-------|---------|-------|----|
| Articuno | Kanto Room | Kanto Slate | **Shining Pearl only** | 70 | Snow Cloak |
| Zapdos | Kanto Room | Kanto Slate | **Shining Pearl only** | 70 | Static |
| Moltres | Kanto Room | Kanto Slate | **Shining Pearl only** | 70 | Flame Body |
| Mewtwo | Genome Room | Genome Slate | Both versions | 70 | Unnerve |

**Critical details:**
- **NOT shiny locked** -- all Ramanas Park legendaries can be shiny (confirmed)
- Slates are consumed on use regardless of catch outcome -- if you KO or flee, the Slate is gone and you must buy another
- Kanto Slate is Shining Pearl exclusive -- BD players must trade for the birds
- Genome Slate (Mewtwo) is available in both versions
- These legendaries come with their Hidden Abilities (unlike many other games)

### B.8 Breeding-Only Pokemon

Several Gen 1 species are only obtainable by catching their evolutions and breeding:

- **Starters** (Bulbasaur, Charmander, Squirtle): Available in Underground hideaways directly, no breeding needed
- **Baby Pokemon**: Pichu (breed Pikachu), Cleffa (breed Clefairy), Igglybuff (breed Jigglypuff) -- also available directly

## Section C: Collector Walkthrough and Transfer

### C.1 BDSP Progression for Gen 1 Collection

**Pre-National Dex (main story):**
1. Surface routes provide: Zubat, Geodude, Onix, Machop, Abra, Magikarp, Psyduck, Tentacool, Gastly, Ponyta, Cleffa, Goldeen
2. Grand Underground hideaways have limited pre-National Dex Pokemon (mostly Sinnoh native species)
3. Eevee gift from Bebe (requires National Dex)

**Post-National Dex (where 90% of Gen 1 collecting happens):**
1. Underground hideaways unlock the bulk of Gen 1 species across all biomes
2. Trophy Garden rotation provides Porygon, Meowth, Chansey, Eevee, Clefairy, Jigglypuff
3. Great Marsh adds Exeggcute, Kangaskhan, Paras
4. Swarms add Pidgey, Cubone
5. Ramanas Park provides Articuno/Zapdos/Moltres (SP) and Mewtwo (both)
6. Poke Radar becomes available for shiny hunting

**Breeding required for:**
- Hitmonlee, Hitmonchan (from Tyrogue)
- Some baby forms if not directly encountered
- Evolution chains (Nidoking from Nidorino, etc.)

### C.2 Poke Radar Chaining for Shinies

The Poke Radar is BDSP's strongest unique feature for collectors. Key targets for Gen 1 shiny hunters:

**Best Poke Radar targets** (species available in grass routes post-National Dex):
- Pikachu (Trophy Garden)
- Eevee (Trophy Garden -- chain for shiny Eevee, then evolve into any Eeveelution)
- Ponyta / Rapidash (Routes 206, 211)
- Magikarp (Route 226 surfing -- note: Radar only works in grass, not water)

**Radar limitations:**
- Only works in tall grass (no caves, water, or Underground)
- The Shiny Charm does not affect Radar odds
- Chains can randomly break (RNG factor)
- Post-National Dex routes in the Battle Zone (Routes 225-230) have the widest species variety

### C.3 What BDSP Offers That Other Games Don't

**Unique to BDSP:**
1. **BDSP origin mark** -- the only way to get this specific mark
2. **Non-shiny-locked Ramanas Park legendaries** -- Mewtwo, Articuno, Zapdos, Moltres can all be shiny. Compare: LGPE shiny locks Mewtwo (static encounter); SwSh doesn't have the birds/Mewtwo wild; DPPt didn't have Ramanas Park
3. **Hidden Ability legendaries from Ramanas Park** -- Articuno with Snow Cloak, Zapdos with Static (HA), Moltres with Flame Body, Mewtwo with Unnerve
4. **Poke Radar at 1/99 odds** -- one of the best grass-encounter shiny methods in the series
5. **Gen 4 ribbon set** -- daily ribbons, contest ribbons, and purchasable ribbons that mirror original DPPt but with easier access
6. **Safari Ball Pokemon from Great Marsh** -- limited selection but a legal Safari Ball source

**What BDSP does NOT uniquely offer:**
- No Apricorn Balls (use HGSS for those)
- No Marks/Titles (SwSh exclusive)
- No Gmax forms (SwSh exclusive)
- No overworld shiny visibility (LGPE/PLA feature)
- No Dynamax Adventures (SwSh Crown Tundra)
- Ball selection is entirely standard
- Every Gen 1 species available here is also available in SwSh or SV with more features

### C.4 Pre-Transfer Checklist

Before sending BDSP Pokemon to HOME:

- [ ] **Ribbons:** Collect all available ribbons (especially daily ribbons -- 7 days for the full set, contest ribbons, Battle Tower ribbon)
- [ ] **Friendship:** Max friendship for Best Friends Ribbon and Footprint Ribbon
- [ ] **Ability:** Use Ability Patch if you want HA before transfer (also available in other games)
- [ ] **EVs:** Train EVs for Effort Ribbon (can also EV train in destination game)
- [ ] **Moves:** Check for any BDSP-exclusive move tutor moves (limited -- most move tutors from Platinum are absent in BDSP)
- [ ] **Ball choice:** Verify the ball is what you want -- cannot be changed after catch
- [ ] **Shiny status:** If shiny hunting via Radar, complete before transfer obviously

### C.5 Honest Assessment

**BDSP is low-priority for Gen 1 collectors.** The game faithfully recreates DPPt but lacks the collector-rich features of HGSS (Apricorn Balls), SwSh (Marks, Dynamax Adventures), or even LGPE (catch combos, overworld shinies). The main reasons to source Gen 1 Pokemon from BDSP are:

1. You want the BDSP origin mark specifically
2. You want to shiny hunt legendaries at full odds in Ramanas Park (not shiny locked)
3. You want HA legendaries (Ramanas Park gives these directly)
4. You want the Gen 4 ribbon set on a Pokemon
5. You enjoy Poke Radar chaining

For a pure Gen 1 living dex, BDSP is redundant with SwSh/SV. For a ribbon master project, BDSP adds a meaningful set of ribbons. For shiny legendary hunting, BDSP's full-odds encounters are worse than SwSh Dynamax Adventures (1/100) but the Pokemon are guaranteed not shiny locked.
